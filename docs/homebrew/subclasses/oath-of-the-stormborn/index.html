Oath of the Stormborn

Description​

Paladins who swear the Oath of the Stormborn wield the fury of the heavens, channeling thunder and lightning as both shield and hammer. They are relentless warriors of the storm, striking down foes with divine force and protecting those caught in the tempest of war.

Tenets of the Stormborn

Thunder’s Wrath: Let your enemies tremble before the storm’s fury. Strike down oppressors with righteous force.

Storm’s Shelter: Like the eye of the hurricane, you are a protector. Stand firm as a shield for the innocent.

Unbreakable Tempest: The storm does not yield; neither shall you. Remain steadfast in your convictions.

Sky’s Judgment: Let the wicked know there is no escape from the storm’s retribution.

Features:

  • 3rd Level - Stormborn Oath Spells
  • 3rd Level - Channel Divinity
  • 7th Level - Thunder's Fury
  • 7th Level - Aura of the Tempest
  • 15th Level - Stormforged Arsenal
  • 20th Level - Ascension of the Stormlord

3rd Level - Stormborn Oath Spells​

Paladin Level | Spells

  • 3rd | Thunderwave, Fog Cloud
  • 5th | Shatter, Gust of Wind
  • 9th | Call Lightning, Fly
  • 13th | Storm Sphere, Freedom of Movement
  • 17th | Destructive Wave (thunder/lightning), Control Winds

3rd Level - Channel Divinity​

Thunderbound Weapon (Passive)​

You summon a weapon bound to your soul, a conduit of the storm’s might.

  • You can summon or dismiss your Thunderbound Weapon as a bonus action.
  • Your Thunderbound Weapon must be a warhammer, battleaxe, greataxe, or maul.
  • You can spend 1 hour binding a magical weapon to make it your Thunderbound Weapon.
  • When using Divine Smite, you can choose to deal radiant, thunder, or lightning damage, the damage type is chosen whenever you use the Thunderbound weapon Channel divinity. This also applies to Improved Divine Smite.
  • The weapon deals 1d8 Additional thunder or lighting damage.

Stormcaller’s Bolt (Active)​

You can cast Witch Bolt without expending a spell slot. This version:

  • Deals thunder damage instead of lightning.
  • Automatically casts at the highest level available for a Paladin spell slot you have (up to 5th).

7th Level - Thunder's Fury​

Your Thunderbound Weapon becomes magical for the purpose overcoming resistance.

7th Level - Aura of the Tempest​

The storm’s energy crackles around you, offering both protection and retribution.

You and allies within 10 feet gain resistance to lightning and thunder damage.

Additionally once per turn when an enemy within 10 feet of you attacks an ally, you can, deal 1d8 thunder damage to the attacker and push the attacker 10 feet away from you.

At 18th level, the aura increases to 30 feet.

15th Level - Stormforged Arsenal​

Your Thunderbound Weapon becomes an extension of the storm’s fury gaining the following benefits:

  • It gains the Thrown property and magically returns to your hand after an attack.
  • You can Divine Smite on ranged (thrown) attacks made with your thunderbound weapon
  • Versatile weapons (warhammer, battleaxe) have a thrown range of 30/60 feet.
  • Heavy weapons (greataxe, maul) have a thrown range of 15/30 feet.

20th Level - Ascension of the Stormlord​

For 1 minute, you become a living storm, gaining the following benefits:

  • You gain a flying speed of 60 feet and hover.
  • Storm Strikes: Once per turn, when you hit a creature with your thunderbound weapon, you can cause a concussive thunder to explode on the target. You force them to make constitution saving thrown. On a failed save they attacked is incapacitated until the start of your next turn. If they fail the save by more than 5 they are instead stunned. Additional  a bolt of lightning arcs to another creature of your choice within 30 feet, forcing the creature to make dexterity saving thrown, dealing 4d10 lightning damage on a failed save or half as much damage on a save.
  • You also become immune to lighting and thunder damage for the duration

Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest or expend a 5th-level spell slot.
