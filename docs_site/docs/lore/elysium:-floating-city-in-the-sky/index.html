Elysium: Floating City in the Sky

The Beginning​

And so it came to pass that the evils of long-past would not rest idly by. Arising from the crater of Dockers Bay, the entity which had wrought its destruction upon the town arose once again, and headed toward the survivors' encampment. Hastily were plans made, and the evacuation of all that remained was underway.

With groups of adventurers splitting to achieve differing objectives, ultimately the entity was stalled in a climactic battle, and in its haste, rent its destructive energy prematurely.

Having successfully evacuated North of the Cloud Peaks into the Wood of Sharp Teeth, the survivors once again set camp. In the wake of the comet entity's second destructive event, several changes had been observed:

First, the light of the comet in the sky dimmed to a level in which it is only just barely visible in the night sky.

Second, unlike in the wake of the destruction of Dockers Bay, there seemed to have been very little residual energy in the second crater - Almost no Nightwalkers or shadow entities of any kind.

Lastly, as the comet's light dimmed, something appeared to break off of it, hurtling across the sky like a meteor before impacting the ground some distance to the East of the new encampment. The expelled mass from the comet turned out to be a device created in the ancient Nethereil Empire by the Magos of Xiandlier Enclave. The floating enclaves of Netheril, powered by Mythallars, ultimately fell when the Mythallars, not wholly unexpectedly, were destroyed in what became known as Karsus's Folly.

Within the unique device was the creator himself, Magos Esra. Finding himself a man out of time, he was met by several adventurers from the encampment who had come to investigate the meteor which had fallen from the sky. The Magos explained that his enclave had been destroyed by the elder-evil, the Star of Ice and Hate, Delban. His description of the entity which came to do its master's bidding was identical to that of the Comet Entity which had destroyed Dockers Bay, and had sought to destroy the encampment at the Cloud Peaks. Seeking the power of the Mythallars of Xiandlier, it also took interest in the Magos' experiment, and ripped it straight from the enclave's core as well, taking the Magos along with it.

Now, having learned of the fate of the Netheril Empire, and much of the history that had passed in the thousand years or so since his own time, Esra seeks to rebuild his device, improve upon it, and re-create an enclave like those of ancient Netheril. At the invitation of the adventurers who had come across him after crashing into the forest, he appeared above the encampment with the beginnings of a new enclave: a mass of land and earth approximately 800 x 800 ft. He suggested that if he were able to get more power to the crystal core which sustained its floating and traveling capability, that it might be expanded upon in the future.

To that end, he invited the people of the encampment knowing they, too, had suffered at the destructive hand of Delban, to come aboard the nascent enclave, and stay there. In exchange, he asked that they render their aid should the floating island come under attack, or for the occasional task that might be required to maintain its integrity. He added that all were welcome to stay on the island, so long as they also did not interfere with his work, nor the operations of the crystal entombed at the core of the island mass.

The Realm Wanders​

The island, which Esra named Elysium (for the hubris of the Netherese would naturally lead one to name their island for a Celestial Plane), was designed specifically to travel. It would not do to have it remain stationery over the Cloud Peaks once the refugees of Docker's Bay had come to settle the floating landmass.

Waterdeep​

Having developed on the island a bit, and established a permanent residence and facilities, the new residents of Elysium decided that the island should set sail for Waterdeep. After several days of travel, Elysium arrived just over the horizon, staying fifty miles outside the border of Waterdeep.

The city's denizens, quite suspicious and wary of the sudden appearance of a giant floating landmass, resorted to keeping scouts on patrol regularly. Never was Elysium left without the watching eyes of Waterdeep scouts. While the adventurers of Elysium were regarded with mistrust and suspicion in Waterdeep, they had not as yet been barred from entering or conducting business there. Tensions however were high, and the slightest provocation, perceived or not, may tilt relations into open hostility.

The Annexation of Elysium​

For a time, Elysium and Waterdeep coexisted relatively peacefully, though never without tension. Helping Waterdeep with some of its contracts helped the Elysians gain rapport, but it wasn't long before those in power found another avenue of taxation in the rich adventurers of the floating island, and Ramiel's Crusade in the east becoming an increasing threat provided yet more (albeit convenient) reason for Waterdeep to annex Elysium.

The annexation began cordially, if not gladly. While the adventurers weren't happy to have to pay taxes -- some even going so far as to find every way possible to avoid doing so -- many did see the value in such a close alignment with the Lords' Alliance. But it wasn't long before even that thin connection began to break down.

Word soon spread among Waterdeep's nobility of the new ward and the rich adventurers inhabiting it. Not to be outdone by these overpaid mercenaries who merely played at being nobles, but who clearly didn't see value of the land under their very feet, rich Waterdhavians began purchasing it and moving in. With the influx of people came an influx of problems, culminating in the attempted murder of a noble in the middle of the Cloud Top Inn.

Elysium Regains Independence​

As the island further developed and began to move again, relations with Waterdeep were strained and Gray Wardens were placed as a proxy for the city staff to alleviate tensions and allow the island to roam without inciting diplomatic incidents. However, this was but a band aid, and relations with Waterdeep were stretched to the breaking point, resulting in Waterdeep releasing Elysium and allow it to become its own independent island.

Negotiations were conducted among the land owners within the Cloud Top Inn about what should be done for the new government, and it was decided that an unusual form of government known as a democracy would be set up on the island. So Thorlas, Lord Commander of the Gray Wardens and Noble Lord of Waterdeep, acting as the city-state's representative, officialized the documents set up during the discussion and independence was declared, resulting in the withdrawal of the Warden peacekeepers and the passing of the torch to the new government.

A woman by the name of Phoenix was elected to Mayor, with a supporting council comprised of Kaynes, Jerry, Alina, Alkafyn, Jack, and Cabbage. However they were not the only ones involved in the city's independence. Bryan, Clark Aiwon, Ajani, Loops Silva, Pon, Anastasia, and Vapor, among a number of other land owners, were present as well and contributed to the discussion. Together, they began the long and arduous process of nurturing the fledgling government.
