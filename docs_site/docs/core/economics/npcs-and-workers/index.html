NPCs and Workers

NPCs & Workers​

NPC Crafting Update​

A) Unnamed NPCs can still be hired to make mundane crafts, with minimum/no RP. Unlimited NPCs per mundane craft, although a PC still needs to lead it. You have our blessing, changes will be implemented if this is abused.

B) Named NPC Crafters require RP to be created. What ‘skill level’ this NPC starts with varies from case-to-case depending on the RP being done. Follow the chart below for upgrading rules.

NPC Upgrading​

To allow for Named NPC crafters to upgrade the rarities they can craft. A specific amount of crafts of a certain rarity must be made (listed below) as well as a Wisdom check (made by PC) following the final craft (DCs also listed below).

NPC Crafter Upgrade Requirements​
New TierMinimum # of CraftsWisdom DC
Uncommon20 CommonDC 15
Rare40 UncommonDC18
Very Rare60 RareDC20
Legendary80 Very RareDC22

Notes:

  • Mundane Crafters will never be able to upgrade to common or higher.
  • Link the check in the final craft (whether the check passes or not).
  • Failing the check sends you back to 50% progress.
  • Worker Limit is still 5

Retired PC Crafter Limit​

Players are limited to three retired PC crafters at any one point. All retired PCs may do other allowed forms of downtime, but only three can craft at any one time.

Reason: To prevent people from overpopulating their personal supply of crafters by making and retiring PCs over and over again.

Crafter Rule Change​

If a player leaves the server, their characters can not be used to craft. If a character is retired and their sheet is not posted in ⁠retired-listings, they can not be used to craft. This change also removes the 3 month mandatory check-in between Guild Owner and Employee, because the assumption is the employee can leave the guild whenever they see fit.

Note: If a craft has already been initiated, and the player or players involved in the craft leave the server, the craft will be completed on schedule.

Worker Rights​

When you are using a PC to craft (everyone aside from the 'starter'), you MUST pay them each a minimum of 25 GP per day of crafting. This includes your own PCs, if applicable.
