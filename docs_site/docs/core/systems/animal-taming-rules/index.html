Animal Taming Rules

Animal Taming Rules​

Overview​

  • A PC may attempt to tame animals over an extended period of time.

Taming Check​

Check: Wisdom (Animal Handling)

Tame DC: 15 + (2 * CR) (minimum 11)

Time: 1 full day of calm interaction (one day of downtime)

Initial Tame​

Before training begins, the creature must first be made non-hostile through patience, calmness, or courage.

RollResult
Natural 20Start at 25% Bond Progress
SuccessCreature becomes non-hostile and open to training
Fail by 5 or lessYou fail to train the creature, you may retry after 1 day
Fail by 5 or moreYou fail to train the creature, you may retry after 3 days
Natural 1The creature flees or attacks, you may retry after 1 week

Long-Term Bond Training​

Each day requires 8 hours of focused interaction. You can train only one creature at a time. Each success adds your roll to a cumulative total.

RollResult
Natural 20Add roll plus 10% of total progress threshold
SuccessYou Progress your bond with the creature
Fail by 5 or lessYour bond with the creature does not progress
Fail by 5 or moreThe creature refuses training, you may retry after 3 days
Natural 1The creature flees or attacks, you may retry after 1 week

Progress cannot fall below your current bond stage unless broken by neglect or narrative reason.

Bond Progression​

Bond StageProgress Threshold
Non-HostileSuccess on Initial Tame
Sociable10 + (10 * CR)
Basic Commands
Stay. Come. Stop.15 + (15 * CR)
Complex Commands
Dodge. Attack X creature. Hide.20 + (20 * CR)
Mounted / Complex Tasks
Scout X area. Return to X location. Find X person.25 + (25 * CR)

Example: A CR 3 beast needs 80 total progress to reach Complex Commands.

Once a stage is reached, it remains permanent unless broken through RP.

Proper care sustains trust.

Shelter: Will be determined by DM

Luxury Care: Double Daily Cost

Care Factors​

  • One day without care: Lose 10% Progress
  • Two days without care: Creature may flee and reset to 0%
  • Luxury Care: After 3 days of Luxury Care you gain advantage on the next training roll

Tamable Creatures​

  • Beasts & Monstrosities comprised of Beasts (ex: Griffon, Hippogriff)
  • Subject to DM discretion. A number of monsters that may technically fit the criteria may not be tamable for lore reasons, or may otherwise be inaccessible due to where they may be located.
  • Young Griffin (CR 0, ¼, 1)
  • Griffon (CR 2)
  • Owlbear (CR 3)
  • Amphisbaena (CR ½)
  • Peryton (CR 2)
  • Kamadan (CR 4)
  • Death Dog (CR 1)

Limitations​

  • It must be reasonable for the creature to be tamed, per DM discretion.
  • It must make sense within the context of TWR lore, per DM discretion.
  • The creatures you can potentially tame are limited by the CR. In order to have the skill necessary to safely handle a creature, you must be capable of hitting the initial tame DC through just a straight animal handling roll without additional modifiers from items, spells, abilities, etc.

Maximum Tamable CR = (5 + Your Base Animal Handling Modifier) / 2

  • Creatures with legendary or mythic abilities can not be tamed.
  • Creatures with an INT of 1 can not be tamed.
  • Creatures with an INT of 4+ can not be tamed.
  • The initial tame, and subsequent checks, must be RPed for and submitted in transcript.
  • For CR 1+ The initial tame must occur in a mini-quest or RPed targeted hunt run by DM. A DM can not run their own quest if they are attempting to tame a creature.
  • For any creatures intended as a mount, the initial tame must occur in a mini-quest or RPed targeted hunt run by DM. A DM can not run their own quest if they are attempting to tame a creature.
  • Purchasable domesticated mounts are an exception, and can be purchased as normal.
  • No more than 1 creature can be tamed per day.
  • Exceptions may exist for things such as domesticated animals, per discretion of the DM.
  • Only creatures at least one size larger than a PC are mountable.
Owl-Bear Taming Example Steps​
  • Go on a mini quest hosted by a DM.
  • RP to find and convince the Owl-Bear that you are both not food and non hostile.
  • CR3 creature, so DC 30 animal handling check for an adult Owl-Bear.
  • Upon success, it will be non hostile but still aggressive.
  • Cumulative DC of 300. The daily RPed animal handling checks would be added together.
  • With an average roll of 10, with the +10 modifier required to initially tame the creature, it would take 15 days in real life of daily checks and RP to tame the creature.
  • At 75 points, it would become sociable
  • At 150 points, it would accept simple commands
  • At 225 points, it would accept complex commands and barding
  • At 300 points, it would accept complex tasks and be mountable

Use of Tamed Creatures In Combat​

The creature acts on its own initiative. You control it as you would a summon RAW from spells such as Conjure Animals.

Mounted Combat for Tamed Creatures​

  • Because of the inherent danger of things such as meteor swarms, when you are mounted the creature shares your HP.
  • If you fall to zero HP, your mount falls to 1 HP, you are dismounted if on land, and if it is a flying creature it begins to crashland at 60ft/round.
  • For the purposes of movement, the mount replaces your move speed(s).
  • HP when dismounting: When you dismount, if your HP is lower than the mount's HP, the mount will adopt the same current HP as you.
  • If you choose to have the mount attack, take any action, or otherwise treat it as a separate creature, ignore points 1~4 and it will be treated as a normal mount RAW.
Mounted Combatant Feat interaction​

Under this system you can use either RAW and risk damage to the mount or the alternative rules in the Animal Taming doc. If you're using the alternative rules only point 1 of the feat pertaining to advantaged against creatures is applicable, the other two are nullified.

Sale and Transfer of Animals​

  • The recipient must be capable of hitting the initial tame DC to be capable of controlling the creature in any way.
  • By default, the bond level resets to zero. If you are proficient in Animal Handling, it resets to 25%. If you have expertise in Animal Handling, it resets to 50%

Tame Death​

If your tamed creature dies and is revived it will regress one cumulative level. If your tame is regressed past 0 the taming process with restart requiring a new initial tame roll.
