Paragon Systems

Prestige of the Gods​

Prestige of the Gods​

A character who has reached level 20 and achieved the 6 post-20 milestones may choose to undergo a divine transformation in which the gods revert their mind and body to the state of a neophyte adventurer. Starting again at level 1-3, a ‘prestiged’ adventurer may begin again - choosing any new class, feats, or even remodeling their own personal attributes. They retain all knowledge, wealth, and any items they had prior to this.

Upon choosing to undergo the transformation, the adventurer may choose a cosmetic effect to be bestowed upon them. This effect must be proposed by the player, and approved by a DM in the request to prestige the character (in #transcript). Examples of such an effect could include: Flittering sparkles that follow the character, an ethereal halo above their head, or a glowing/changing tattoo.

Paragon Systems: Gestalt and Ascendant​

Gestalts are now considered a Paragon system, with the addition of Ascendants, which operate in a similar way - granting class levels past 20 - without having to reset.

A character’s Paragon stage represents the number of times they have completed a full class level system (finished p20), starting at a value of 1. This number is used to determine experience gain, and for communicating your character’s progress, as seen in “How are Levels Counted”. Such systems and changes can and will be subject to change and active monitoring.

How Levels are Counted​

Levels are counted through the following format A#L#, where A represents your paragon stage, and L represents your remaining levels. For example, a P26 wizard with 10 additional levels from this system would be an A1L10. This can be similarly done with tiers.

This is similarly done with gestalt, in which the format is G#L#, where G represents your Paragon stage, and L represents your current level, up to 26. This can be similarly done with tiers.

Ascendant Paragon​

Characters can now progress past level 20, continuing to gain character levels, being known as Ascendants rather than Gestalts. The character’s XP counter would reset to zero, though they would keep their character levels, proficiency bonus, and P20 rewards. Such Ascendants would proceed to level another class, starting from T1, and gaining an appropriate amount of XP, with the ability to do so again upon completing P20.

Build Rules​

  • To qualify a PC must be fully leveled, meaning level 20 with all post 20 levels completed.

  • Ascendant PCs cannot gain experience from hunts, trials, DM-XP, Transcript-XP, etc. They can only gain experience from RP-XP or Quests.

  • In quests that involve combat, while you will receive XP for the RP, you will receive no XP for the creatures killed.

  • Ascendant hit dice are restricted to the two highest hit dice per level (as opposed to getting all hit dice per level).

  • Rather than gaining the save proficiencies of all classes, Ascendants choose one strong save and one weak save from the options given to them by their classes:

    • Strong Saves: Dex, Con, Wis
    • Weak Saves: Str, Int, Cha

  • Any changes to the locked classes or subclasses are allowed and follow the normal retraining rules.

  • Due to the documentation required for this system, character sheets must be handled in G-Sheets.

  • ASIs stack, but do not stack past the normal cap of 20 unless otherwise specified. Stats may not exceed 30 for any reason.

  • You can not take the same feat multiple times unless otherwise specified. Feats that scale based on level are calculated based on your class levels.

  • Ascendant PC's, May only hunt, trial or delve with PC's who are also Ascendant

  • Spell save DCs and spellcasting follow multi classing rules. Spell Save DCs and Spell Attack Bonuses calculate based on the class providing the spell.

  • Class features that references class levels are self contained for the applicable class and paragon cycle

Stacking​

Class Features, Spell Slots, Proficiencies and Stat Changes generally stack unless otherwise specified.

Exceptions​

The following features do NOT stack when creating a Ascendant PC:

  • Proficiency Bonus
  • Extra Attack
  • Unarmored Defense
  • Duplicated Paladin Auras
  • Movement speed increases from duplicate sources.
  • Bladesong, and other features with limited uses such as rage, do not get doubled buffs but do get a increased number of uses.

Prestige Classes​

The Ascendant Paragon system also includes Prestige Classes, classes that have specific requirements for their use and play, but are generally stronger than the normal classes. Further information can be found at the Prestige Classes page.

Gestalt Paragon​

This is a variant to the typical prestige system, and while it may grant great boons it also has great restrictions as well. When you Gestalt Paragon, you must post in #transcript including both the new and original sheets for reference.

Gestalt Overview​

  • Upon gestalt prestige you can select a class to level alongside your current one.
  • The starting proficiencies, skills, and other benefits are granted to the character.
  • When you level up, both classes level simultaneously, and you gain the benefits from both.
  • Put simply, you have build A which was gestalted, getting added to build B. This is similar to if you had two separate characters who were merged into one.
  • ASIs granted through level ups can be reassigned.
  • This process can be repeated, allowing for a singular character to slowly grow in power over the years as they grow more involved in the world at large.

Cumulative Level

  • Cumulative Level is the total number of levels from all your classes combined.
  • Cumulative Level is used to calculate what trials, hunts, and quests you can be involved in.

Current Level​

  • Current Level is the current base level of the character and calculated as it would be normally, as though you were leveling a normal character.
  • The Current Level is what is used to calculate proficiency bonus, character progression, and RP experience gains. Things that scale based on level use your Current Level.

Gestalt Reversion​

  • At any time a character can revert a gestalt prestige. This will reset the character to a level 1 class of their choice akin to a normal prestige, provide zero cosmetic and stat benefits, and allow the character to once again gain experience from trials, hunts, etc.
  • The character will then be locked out of the gestalt prestige system for 6 months.
  • This can be done at any time and at any level.

Build Rules​

  • All Gestalt PCs begin at level 1

  • Class levels do not mix between Gestalt Prestiges. Each prestige is treated as a unique PC build.

  • To qualify a PC must be fully leveled, meaning level 20 with all post 20 levels completed.

  • Gestalt PCs cannot gain experience from hunts, trials, DM-XP, Transcript-XP, etc. They can only gain experience from RP-XP or Quests.

  • In quests that involve combat, while you will receive XP for the RP, you will receive no XP for the creatures killed.

  • Gestalt PCs may not change their race, background, Racial ASIs, class or subclass choice, or anything else regarding the basis of the character which was set during their initial creation.

  • On level up, the initial class chosen will follow the same build path (for example Fighter Champion) and multi class level split (if applicable) that the character had PRIOR to that prestige.

  • Gestalt hit dice are restricted to the two highest hit dice per level (as opposed to gestalts getting all hit dice per level).

  • Rather than gaining the save proficiencies of all classes, Gestalts choose one strong save and one weak save from the options given to them by their classes:

    • Strong Saves: Dex, Con, Wis
    • Weak Saves: Str, Int, Cha

  • Gestalt PC's may choose to redistribute their ASI's and redo their first class in any way that is within the rules.

  • Due to the documentation required for this system, character sheets must be handled in G-Sheets.

  • ASIs stack, but do not stack past the normal cap of 20 unless otherwise specified. Stats may not exceed 30 for any reason.

  • You can not take the same feat multiple times unless otherwise specified. Feats that scale based on level are calculated based on your Current Level not your Total Level.

  • Gestalt PCs may attune to any item they own, however, Gestalt PCs may not use items which exceed their “Current Level” during hunts or trials with non-Gestalt PCs.

  • Gestalt PCs may choose to join hunts above their Current Level, but not if non-Gestalt PCs are present. This does not affect Body Retrieval Hunts. If a gestalt hunting party is a mix of tiers, they can not recover a body.

  • Spell save DCs and spellcasting follow multi classing rules. Spell Save DCs and Spell Attack Bonuses calculate based on the class providing the spell.

Stacking​

Class Features, Spell Slots, Proficiencies and Stat Changes generally stack unless otherwise specified.

Exceptions​

The following features do NOT stack when creating a Gestalt PC:

  • Proficiency Bonus
  • Extra Attack
  • Unarmored Defense
  • Duplicated Paladin Auras
  • Movement speed increases from duplicate sources.
  • Bladesong, and other features with limited uses such as rage, do not get doubled buffs but do get a increased number of uses.

Paragon Wish Patch:​

tl;dr - Introducing a very arduous method to regain wish through a whole gestalt prestige and questline using a Post20 Blessing/Boon Slot.

Requirements​

  1. Paragon into a spellcasting class that also learns wish
  2. Complete self RP and DM-led quests (at least 4) throughout your prestige that meet the following guidelines
    1. Integrate with an existing group of worshipers for a particular deity
    2. Receive character references from leadership within the church/group
    3. Reach a level that would otherwise grant you wish from your prestige class
    4. Travel to the chosen deity’s home plane and petition their aid directly with offerings and references prepared
    5. Donate at least 2 items to the god directly, sacrificing them to prove your devotion. One item must be of legendary power and the second must be of great personal importance to your PC.
    6. Perform a quest for that god directly, run by a Dungeon master and approved by at least 2 more DMs or LMs

Regaining Wish​

Once all of the requirements have been met and your PC is eligible for a Post20 Boon/Blessing Slot, the God will decide whether or not to grant you the Blessing of Forgiveness:

Blessing of Forgiveness - This blessing removes one existing instance of permanent loss of wish. Once a creature has received this blessing, they can no longer receive it from the same deity.

Note: It may not be possible to convince any other gods to grant this blessing or you may attract significant attention from your previous god if so. It would require unfathomable time and effort to regain wish a second time.

Gestalt and Ascension Interaction:​

Gestalt or Ascension can be chosen when a character finishes p20 or completes a paragon cycle (finishes p20 on another class system). In doing so, a character may choose to Gestalt as a way to prestige and change through their class choices, though would still level according to their paragon cycle number.

For example, a P3L0 (has finished this cycle but has not started a new one) may choose to Gestalt, and become a G4L1, where they may again choose to move back into ascension after leveling to p20.
