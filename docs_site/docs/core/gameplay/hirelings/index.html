Hirelings​

For those of you running organizations and have had questions regarding growth rates and the like, we now have a official system in place to manage wages, growth rates, CRs of hired staff, etc. It will soon be integrated into a larger document for readability and the like, but this is the back end system for folks to use and take a look at. If you have any questions, please ping a <@&879237772047712326> in <#819298824602255360>. If you are running a organization that has NPCs, please note this does not include crafting NPCs or PC-NPCs, please ping a <@&879237772047712326> in <#823408859444019240> so we can add your information to the sheet and get it in <#817297426335006751> to make it official.

Also, just as a general addendum, we’re trying to shift to better tracking methods so we have a better grasp on how everything is working together regarding player orgs, the economy, crafting, etc, so we can bring a better experience to you fine folks. The more we know, the better we can make informed decisions regarding the macro systems.

Also also, the system is subject to minor edits as it gets dialed in.

Hireling Update​

Hireling Addendum, minor update Team: @Loremaster

Creature type is restricted to humanoid (already a thing but clarified) Statblock does not nessicary determine appearance, alignment, etc (already a thing but clarified) All of them by default speak common (new) If you have excess high CR creatures you dont want to use, you can use that “slot” for a creature of a lower CR. So for example, if you don’t want to use CR 8 creatures due to costs (or whatever), you can use a CR 3 creature in its place instead. (new) Creatures that use unique named items, such as Steel, can not be used (new)

Hireling Update 2​

Team involved: @Loremaster

Hello lurkers!

This update is an addendum to the previously released Hireling spreadsheet for NPC pricing. Utilizing this, you will have guidelines with which you may hire NPCs over time, up to a predetermined maximum amount, for whatever individual, group, or organizational needs you have. If there is confusion on how this works, please use the attached spreadsheet, or ask a @Loremaster.

Contracting NPC Requirements:

Maximum CR that can be hired as a single unit is capped at CR 20

Total CR across all hired units is capped at CR = 30

Hiring occurs weekly, using a point deduction system (Hire Points)

Points accrue at a rate of 1/week from the time interest is expressed, and equate to the Total CR that can be used to hire units

Point deductions are based against the CR being hired.

Example: Player currently has total of 5 Hire Points. The player can Hire any combination of humanoid creatures less than or equal to the total CR of 5.

Spreadsheet for CR to Hire Points breakdown can be seen here: https://docs.google.com/file/d/1Qv2-Cna8BUpkpW0geMdIO68BIL-coSrd/edit?usp=docslist\_api&filetype=msexcel

Please submit these hire requests to transcript and ping a @Loremaster for approval.

Attunement and Magic Items for Hirelings Hirelings can equip and attune to magic items provided to them by their employer based upon their CR, as shown below:

  • CR 1/8 - CR 1/2: Common (no attunement)

  • CR 1 - CR 4: Uncommon (attunement up to 1 item)

  • CR 5 - 10: Rare (attunement up to 2 items)

  • CR 11 - 16: Very Rare (attunement up to 3 items)

  • CR 17+: Legendary (attunement up to 3 items)

If the hirelings statblock has an armor or weapon listed, they are treated as proficient with all weapons or armor of that same type (e.g. leather - light armor, Shortsword - martial weapons). If you have questions or concerns, please me know, and I will be working with existing groups/organizations to get you switched over to this new framework

From #Patch-Notes​

Much discussion and collaboration has gone into the merger of Hireling system with the previously used Organizations system. They will now be brought under one framework, with some added rules for how groups and organizations will work under this system, how hirelings can be equipped with items from their employers, and some promising prospects for future updates.

Individual & Group Hirelings

The ability for individuals and established groups to hire NPCs to augment their personal or collaborative efforts will be broken into one of two categories, with slightly different considerations for each. Hirelings can be reskinned to represent any humanoid race or alignment, and can speak common by default.

Contracting Requirements (Individual)

Hiring updates occur once per week, using a point deduction system (Hire Points)

Points accrue at a rate of 1/week, requiring an approved post from a Loremaster These points are logged via ⁠transcript and the total equates to the Total CR that can be used to hire units for that update.

Point deductions are based on the CR being hired. CR 0 units count as CR 1/8 for this, there is a graph that breaks down these numbers in more detail.

Individuals contracting NPCs will use the 'Deployed Monthly' rate, as opposed the normal Daily/Monthly rate. These expenses are logged via ⁠transaction-log after the update is approved by a Loremaster. These expenses occur at the 1st of every month.

Maximum CR that can be hired as a single unit is capped at CR 20

Total CR that can be hired is capped at CR 20

The screenshot below shows a week-by-week breakdown of how hire points can be spent on the corresponding CR.

Contracting Requirements (Group/Organizational)

Hiring updates occur once per week, using a point deduction system (Hire Points)

Points accrue based upon the modifiers calculated for your group, from 1-10, requiring an approved post from a Loremaster. These points are logged via ⁠transcript and the total equates to the Total CR that can be used to hire units for that update.

Publicity (popularity, reputation, goodwill)

Accessibility (access, reach)

Logistics (training, supplies, budget)

Opportunity (job security, importance, need)

Temp buffs (quests/significant events)

Groups/Organizations consisting of multiple PCs behave as a single ‘entity’ for the sake of point accumulation and spending. Updates will be logged with a single PC representing the group.

Point deductions are based on the CR being hired. CR 0 units count as CR 1/8 for this, there is a graph that breaks down these numbers in more detail.

Groups/Organizations contracting NPCs will use the normal 'Monthly' rate. These expenses are logged via ⁠transaction-log after the update is approved by a Loremaster. These expenses occur at the 1st of every month.

Maximum CR that can be hired as a single unit is capped at CR 20
