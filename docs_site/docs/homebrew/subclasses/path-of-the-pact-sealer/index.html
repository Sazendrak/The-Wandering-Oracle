Path of the Pact Sealer

Path of the Pact Sealer​

Many barbarians never concern themselves with the intricacies of magic, leaving others around them to cast their fancy spells and incantations. A Pact Sealer is no different, however enlisting the aid of a patron to grant them magical capabilities similar to a warlock. These barbarians know as much or as little about these spells as they wish, with their main concern being the power this magic grants them to crush their opponents on the battlefield.

Features:

  • 3rd Level - Pact Magic
  • 3rd Level - Raging Focus
  • 3rd Level - Pact Resilience
  • 6th Level - Rage of Hadar
  • 10th Level - Eldritch Invocations
  • 14th Level - Eldritch Frenzy

3rd Level - Pact Magic​

At 3rd level, you augment your combat techniques with the ability to cast spells. See chapter 10 of the Player’s Handbook for the general rules of spellcasting and chapter 11 of the Player’s Handbook for the warlock spell list.

Cantrips​

You learn two cantrips of your choice from the warlock spell list. You learn an additional warlock cantrip of your choice at 10th level.

Spell Slots​

The Pact Sealer Spellcasting table shows how many spell slots you have. The table also shows what the level of those slots is; all of your spell slots are the same level. To cast one of your warlock spells of 1st level or higher, you must expend a spell slot. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a short or long rest.

For example, when you are 8th level, you have two 2nd-level spell slots. To cast the 1st-level spell witch bolt, you must spend one of those slots, and you cast it as a 2nd-level spell.

Spells Known of 1st Level and Higher:

At 3rd level, you know two 1st-level spells of your choice from the warlock spell list.

The Spells Known column of the Pact Sealer Spellcasting table shows when you learn more warlock spells of your choice of 1st level and higher. A spell you choose must be of a level no higher than what’s shown in the table’s Slot Level column for your level. When you reach 13th level, for example, you learn a new warlock spell, which can be 1st, 2nd, or 3rd level.

Additionally, when you gain a level in this class, you can choose one of the warlock spells you know and replace it with another spell from the warlock spell list, which also must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

Spellcasting Ability​

Your spellcasting ability is charisma for your warlock spells, so you use your charisma whenever a spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your charisma modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a warlock spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

3rd Level - Raging Focus​

At 3rd level, while raging, you are able to use any simple or martial weapon you are holding as a focus for your Pact Sealer spells

In addition, you are able to cast any spell granted by Pact Magic while raging. Doing so requires a successful constitution saving throw against your spell save DC. Starting at 11th level, you can also concentrate on spells granted by Pact Magic.

3rd Level - Pact Resilience​

Determined by the patron you struck a deal with, you are able to gain an additional damage resistance while raging starting at 3rd level.

6th Level - Rage of Hadar​

Starting at 6th level when you rage, once before your rage ends you can use a bonus action to cast the Arms of Hadar spell at the highest level available to you on the Pactsealer table without expending a spell slot.

10th Level - Eldritch Invocations​

Starting at 10th level, you may choose 2 of the following invocations. You can choose a third invocation at 14th level. Whenever you gain a level in this class, you may change one of the invocations you selected for another.

Enraged Casting​

When you roll damage for a Pact Magic spell you cast while raging, you can add your rage damage modifier to the damage roll.

Pact Bound Weapons​

While you are raging, you can replace the bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage type of your weapon attacks with that related to your patron.

Otherworldly Grip​

You cannot be disarmed against your will, and you have advantage on checks made to grapple another creature. Creatures have disadvantage on checks made to escape your grapples.

Boon of the Raven​

While raging, you gain a flying speed of 30 feet.

Evolving Resilience​

When you take damage of the type corresponding to your patron, you can use your reaction to instead become immune to the damage until the start of your next turn. You can do this a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus before taking a long rest.

Devil's Sight​

You gain Darkvision out to a range of 120ft. Additionally, magical darkness does not impede your darkvision.

Horrid Revitalization​

As an action, you can bolster your natural regeneration. Doing so will cause you to regain hit points equal to 5 + your constitution modifier at the start of each of your turns for 1 minute. You cannot do so again until you finish a long rest.

Supernatural Strength​

You gain proficiency in the athletics and acrobatics skills.

Blinding Speed​

You can cast haste once using a pact slot. You can’t do so again until you finish a long rest.

Unholy Smite​

You can cast Blinding Smite once without expending a spell slot. You regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Dimensional Instability​

You can cast Blink once without expending a spell slot. You regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

14th Level - Eldritch Frenzy​

When you score a critical hit against an enemy, you can recover one of your pact slots. You cannot restore another slot in this way until you finish a short rest
