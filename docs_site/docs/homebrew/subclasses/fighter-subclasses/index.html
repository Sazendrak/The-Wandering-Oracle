Fighter Subclasses

Blood Knight​

The blood knight is the title given to an eldritch knight who chooses to delve into the taboo discipline of hemomancy, or the magic of controlling blood and life force. These warriors thrive in the heart of battle where the blood flows thickest, using it to enhance their offensive and defensive capabilities.

Combat Hemomancy​

Starting at 3rd level when you choose this archetype, you gain access to a series of hemomantic abilities that allow you to use the blood of your enemies to your advantage.

When you damage a creature that has blood within 30 feet of you with a weapon attack, you gain a special die called a blood die, which is a D6.  You can have a number of these dice up to a maximum equal to your proficiency bonus. Creatures that do not have blood do not generate blood dice, and you do not gain blood dice from attacks that are modified by blood dice.

You can expend blood dice to perform a number of hemomantic abilities described at the end of this features description.

Each time a hemomantic ability asks you to expend your blood dice, you can choose how many you want to roll and gain a benefit based on what you rolled.

Hemomantic Abilities​

Combat Regeneration. At the start of your turn, you can spend blood dice to gain temporary hit points. Roll the dice you spend, add them together, and gain temporary hit points equal to the total, replacing any temporary hit points you already have.

Crimson Strike. You can use the Attack action to make a special melee attack, a crimson strike. If you're able to make multiple attacks with the Attack action, this attack replaces one of them. You brandish the weapon used in the technique and make a melee attack with it against one creature within 15 feet of you. On a hit, the target takes magical piercing, bludgeoning, or slashing damage equal to the total of the blood dice you spent.

Blood Shell. When you are hit by an attack, you can use your reaction to spend blood dice. Roll the dice you spend, add them together, and increase your AC by the amount equal to the total rolled against that attack.

Blood Clotting. As a bonus action, you can spend blood dice to throw blood onto a surface at a range of 15 feet and have it stick in a 25 ft. square. Any creature that starts its turn in that area or that enters that area during its turn must make a Strength saving throw with a DC of 10 + the amount you roll on your blood dice (A maximum DC of 8 + proficiency bonus + Strength or Dexterity modifier). On a failed save, the creature's speed is reduced to zero until the start of their next turn.

Sense Life​

At 7th level, you gain advantage on wisdom (perception) checks to spot and track enemies that have blood in their veins. In addition, your ability to sense blood can be so precise that you have advantage wisdom (insight) checks to tell if someone is lying to you.

Blood-Fueled Overdrive​

At 10th level, when you use your indomitable feature, you can expend one blood die and add it to the roll.

Hemomantic Precision​

At 15th level, you have a very tight control over blood you spill. You can re-roll one blood die once whenever you roll them for a hemomantic ability.

Bloody Tyrant​

At 18th level, when you hit a creature with an attack and have the maximum number of blood dice, you can expend them all to force the creature to make a Constitution saving throw with a DC of 8 + proficiency bonus + Strength or Dexterity modifier. On a failed save, the creature is paralyzed for 1 minute. At the end of each of its turns, the paralyzed target must make a Constitution saving throw at the end of each of its turns. On a successful save, this paralyzing effect ends.

Daredevil​

The Daredevil fighter thrives on style, versatility and unpredictably. Their use of different weapon types makes them dangerous to any foe and they gain more confidence the more stylish their fighting is.

Daredevil impulse​

Starting at 3rd level, every time you make an attack of a type different from the previous attack in the current combat, you gain one Style point. You have a maximum amount of style points equal to your proficiency bonus. When you roll Initiative your Style points reset to 0.

Attack types are classified as: Melee weapon attack, Ranged weapon attack and thrown attacks.

Style Points can be used to power your Flairs.

Flairs:​

  • Wide attack: If you hit with a melee attack, you may spend 2 Style points to deal melee damage equal to half your Fighter level to all foes within 5 feet of your target.
  • Laser Focus: Spend 2 Style Points to gain advantage on your next attack roll.
  • Range Extender: You can add 10 feet to any ranged or thrown weapon's normal range for every point spent
  • Rush: Gain 10 feet of movement speed for each Style Point spent until the end of your turn.
  • Burst (4th level and up): As a bonus action, Spend 3 Style points to make a weapon attack.

Hotswap​

At 3rd level, whenever you make an attack roll with a weapon, you can sheathe it and draw another in the same action. You can only do this once per action.

Fight or Flight​

From 7th level you have a choice between staying in action and fighting defensively or moving around or to continue as is.  As a bonus action you can choose to either fight or flee from combat.

  • Fight: As a Bonus action you can give yourself a Bonus to your AC equal to the amount of Style Points you spend. This bonus lasts until the end of your next turn or until you are incapacitated. While Fight is active you cannot gain more Style points.
  • Flight: You can spend 2 Style points to disengage, you can't be targeted by opportunity attacks. You gain an additional 10 feet of movement until the end of your turn for every additional Style Point spent.

Double Trouble​

You can choose an additional Fighting Style.

Additionally, using the Disengage, Dodge or Dash actions also builds your Style chain. For example: If you use a dodge action and make an attack on your next turn or vice versa you also gain a Style Point

Wild Rush​

Starting at 15th level, when an enemy within 30 feet of you gets incapacitated or restrained you can use 2 Style Points as an reaction to dash straight toward the enemy. You move 10 feet for every point spent (minimum 2 points) and can spend additional Style for added Rush movement. You can  sheath your current weapon and draw another as part of the dash and make a single attack against that target.

Inferno Trigger​

From 18th level, once per short rest you can expend 6 Style points as an action to trigger your Inferno action.

Inferno action: Can attack 6 times, ignore the loading and ammunition properties if the weapon has it. Any Critical hit deals an additional 10 damage of the weapons damage type. These attacks don't provide any Style points

Deadeye​

The embodiment of grit, luck, and a well-placed shot, fighters who choose this martial archetype strive to emulate paradigms of accuracy. Deadeyes generally prefer fighting from comfortable ranges, employing the use of crossbows, firearms, and even throwing knives if a situation calls for them.

Perfect Sixth​

Once per attack action beginning at 3rd level, when you make a ranged weapon attack roll against a creature, you can add a d6 to the result. You can add the d6 before or after seeing the results of the attack roll, but before the effects are applied. If the result of the d6 is a 6, you severely injure your target. Roll on the severe injury table to see the effect of the injury.

Line ‘em Up​

At 7th level, you become a master at hitting successive targets. When you take the attack action on your turn, each time you target a different creature than any other you’ve targeted this turn, you gain a cumulative +1 bonus to the attack roll.

Gambit​

At 10th level, when you roll initiative, you can gambit a d4, d6, d8, or d10. Roll the chosen die and subtract it from your initiative. Until the end of your next turn, every creature below you in initiative takes extra damage when hit with your ranged attack rolls equal to the chosen die.

Duelist’s Aim​

At 15th level, when you use the attack action to make a ranged weapon attack and you hit at least three consecutive attacks, your third attack inflicts a severe injury.

High Noon​

At 18th level, you are adept at ending multiple targets at once. As an action, choose any number of targets that you can see within a cone the size of your weapon’s short range, up to 60 feet. You may make one ranged weapon attack against each target you chose inflicting the Dead Shot severe injury with each of them. You may use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all uses on a long rest.

Raven Knight​

A Raven Knight is fearsome in battle, fusing martial prowess with strange abilities gifted by the Raven Queen. Many Raven Knights work alone, though some may find a small band of companions to aid them in battle, and some may even command and lead entire armies into battle. Masters of death, Raven Knights seek to protect those who cannot protect themselves from undeath and the afterlife. Powerful Raven Knights may even become champions of the Raven Queen, and thus champions of the battlefield.

Knowledge of Death​

When you choose this Archetype at 3rd level, you gain proficiency in the Investigation and Medicine skills. Additionally, you can make Intelligence (Investigation) and Wisdom (Medicine) checks with advantage when attempting to determine the cause of death of a creature.

Soul Severing​

At 3rd level, when you reduce a creature to 0 hit points you sever the connection of the creatures soul to its body. That creature cannot be turned into an undead. Additionally, as a reaction, when a creature is reduced to 0 hit points within 15ft of you you can empower your next attack with that creatures soul energy. Your next weapon attack deals additional necrotic damage equal to half your fighter level.

Feather Step​

At 7th level, you move with the grace of the raven. Your walking speed increases by 10ft. Additionally, as a bonus action you gain a flight speed equal to your walking speed until the end of your turn, if you end your turn in midair you drop to the ground.

Queens Blessing​

At 10th level, the Raven Queen grants you a blessing. You gain advantage on death saving throws, immunity to the frightened condition, and resistance to necrotic damage.

Deaths Embrace​

At 15th level, when you or an ally critically hit a creature, you can use your reaction to add 3d10 necrotic damage to the attacks damage roll.

Duty in Death​

At 18th level, when you are reduced to 0 hit points or killed outright you can use your reaction to make a weapon attack against the creature that killed you. On a hit you critically hit that creature and regain hit points equal to the damage dealt. You can do this once per long rest.

Revised Arcane Archer​

Arcane Archer Lore​

At 3rd level, you learn magical theory or some of the secrets of nature -- typical for practitioners of this elven martial tradition. You choose to gain proficiency in either the Arcana or the Nature skill, and you choose to learn either the prestidigitation or the druidcraft cantrip.

Arcane Shot​

At 3rd level, you learn to unleash special magical effects with some of your shots. When you gain this feature, you learn two Arcane Shot options of your choice (see Arcane Shot Options below).

Once per turn when you fire an arrow or bolt from any bow as part of the Attack action, you can apply one of your Arcane Shot options to that piece of ammunition. You decide to use the option when the ammunition hits a creature, unless the option doesn't involve an attack roll. You have a number of uses equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses of it when you finish a short or long rest.

You gain an additional Arcane Shot option of your choice when you reach certain levels in this class: 7th, 10th, 15th, and 18th level. Each option also improves when you become a 15th-level fighter.

Arcane Shot Options (Revised)​

The Arcane Shot feature lets you choose options for it at certain levels. The options are presented here in alphabetical order. They are all magical effects, and each one is associated with one of the schools of magic.

If an option requires a saving throw, your Arcane Shot save DC equals 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier.

Banishing Arrow​

You use abjuration magic to try to temporarily banish your target to a harmless location in the Feywild. The creature hit by the arrow must also succeed on a Charisma saving throw or be banished for 1 minute, or until you release the magic as a free action. While banished in this way, its speed is 0, and it is incapacitated. The affected creature can make a Charisma saving throw at the end of their turn, ending the effect on a success. At the end of the duration, the target reappears in the space it vacated or in the nearest unoccupied space if that space is occupied.

After you reach 15th level in this class, a target also takes 4d6 force damage when the arrow hits it.

Beguiling Arrow​

Your enchantment magic causes this arrow to temporarily beguile its target. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 psychic damage, and choose one of your allies within 30 feet of the target. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw, or it is charmed by the chosen ally for 1 minute or until you release the magic as a free action. The target or any creature that can reach it can use its action to remove the arrow with a successful Strength (Athletics) check against your Arcane Shot save DC, ending the effect on a success. Additionally, this effect ends early if the chosen ally attacks the charmed target, deals damage to it, or forces it to make a saving throw.

The psychic damage increases to 4d6 when you reach 15th level in this class.

Bursting Arrow​

You imbue your arrow with force energy drawn from the school of evocation. The arrow detonates after your attack. Immediately after the arrow hits the creature, the target and all other creatures within 10 feet of it take 2d6 force damage each and succeed on a Strength saving throw against your Arcane Shot save DC, or be knocked prone.

The force damage increases to 4d6 when you reach 15th level in this class. (edited)

Enfeebling Arrow​

You weave necromantic magic into your arrow. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 necrotic damage on hit, as well as at the start of each of their turns for 1 minute or until you release the magic as a free action. The target must also succeed on a Constitution saving throw, or the damage dealt by its weapon attacks is halved until the start of your next turn. The target or any creature that can reach it can use its action to remove the arrow with a successful Strength (Athletics) check against your Arcane Shot save DC, ending the effect on a success.

The necrotic damage increases to 4d6 when you reach 15th level in this class.

Grasping Arrow​

When this arrow strikes its target, conjuration magic creates grasping, poisonous brambles, which wrap around the target. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 poison damage, its speed is reduced by 10 feet, and it takes 2d6 slashing damage the first time on each turn it moves 1 foot or more without teleporting. The target or any creature that can reach it can use its action to remove the brambles with a successful Strength (Athletics) check against your Arcane Shot save DC. The brambles last for 1 minute, until you use another option, or until you release the magic as a free action.

The poison damage and slashing damage both increase to 4d6 when you reach 15th level in this class.

Piercing Arrow​

You use transmutation magic to give your arrow an ethereal quality. When you use this option, you don’t make an attack roll for the attack. Instead, the arrow fires forward in a line, which is 5 foot wide and 30 feet long, before disappearing. The arrow passes harmlessly through objects, ignoring cover. Each creature in that line must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes damage as if it were hit by the arrow, plus an extra 2d6 piercing damage. On a successful save, a target takes half as much damage. This option lasts for 1 minute, until you use another option, or until you release the magic as a free action.

The piercing damage increases to 4d6 when you reach 15th level in this class.

Seeking Arrow​

Using divination magic, you grant your arrow the ability to seek out your target, allowing the arrow to curve and twist its path in search of its prey. When you use this option, you don’t make an attack roll for the attack. Instead, choose one creature you have seen in the past minute. The arrow flies toward that creature, moving around corners if necessary and ignoring three-quarters cover and half cover. If the target is within the weapon’s range and there is a path large enough for the arrow to travel to the target, the target must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, it takes damage as if it were hit by the arrow, plus an extra 2d6 force damage, and you learn the target’s current location and distance for 1 hour or until you release the magic as a free action. On a successful save, the target takes half as much damage, and you only learn the targets current location and distance for one round. The target or any creature that can reach it can use its action to remove the arrow with a successful Strength (Athletics) check against your Arcane Shot save DC, ending the effect on a success.

The force damage increases to 4d6 when you reach 15th level in this class. (edited)

Shadow Arrow​

You weave illusion magic into your arrow, causing it to occlude your foe’s vision with shadows. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 psychic damage, and it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be unable to see anything farther than 5 feet away for 1 minute or until you release the magic as a free action. The target or any creature that can reach it can use its action to remove the arrow with a successful Strength (Athletics) check against your Arcane Shot save DC, ending the effect on a success.

The psychic damage increases to 4d6 when you reach 15th level in this class.

Magic Ammunition​

At 7th level, you gain the ability to infuse ammunition with magic. Whenever you fire a nonmagical arrow from a bow, you can make it magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage. The magic fades from the ammunition immediately after it hits or misses its target.

Curving Shot​

At 7th level, you learn how to direct an errant ammunition toward a new target. When you make an attack roll with a magic ammunition and miss, you can use a bonus action to reroll the attack roll against a different target within 60 feet of the original target.

Quick-draw Shot​

Starting at 15th level, your magical archery makes your movements swift. Whenever you hit a target with an Arcane Shot you can use your bonus action to make another ranged weapon attack against the same target using the same weapon.

Eldritch Mastery​

At 18th level, your mastery of blending magic with archery teaches you to infuse your arrows with even greater magic, as well as combining them for greater effect.

Once per attack action, you can infuse a single arrow with two different arcane shot options, excluding piercing shot, having the target be affected by and taking damage from both shots. However when doing this you expend two uses of your Arcane shot.

You have two uses of this ability, and you regain all expended uses of it when you finish a long rest.

Weapon Master​

Deft Grip​

Starting at 3rd level, after you make a weapon attack using the attack action you can use a free action to swap to another weapon.

When you attack with a weapon, you receive a bonus to the attack based on the list below, however you may only benefit from a single bonus once per turn, even if your attack misses; You can however receive an additional bonus if you attack with a different weapon that causes a different type of damage. Ranged weapons do not benefit from Deft Grip, nor do generic “Elemental Weapons” (which collectively count as a single weapon), consumable items that grant additional damage, or spells.

Deft Grip DC: 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Dexterity modifier

Flowing Stance​

At 3rd level, when you successfully hit another creature with a weapon that benefits from the Deft Grip bonus you receive a +1 to hit and to damage for your next attack if your next attack will also benefit from the Deft Grip bonus. These bonuses stack then reset to zero at the end of your turn.

Rapid Strike​

Starting at 7th level, you learn to trade accuracy for swift strikes. If you take the Attack action on your turn and have advantage on an attack roll against against one of the targets, you can forgo the advantage for that roll to make an additional weapon attack against that target, as part of the same action. You can do so no more than once per turn.

Shatter the Horde​

Starting at 10th level you can expend a use of your Action Surge to cause your melee attacks to strike any number of creatures within 5 feet of you, with a separate attack roll for each target. This benefit lasts for a single turn. This does not prevent a second Action Surge from being used on the same turn.

Focused Strike​

Starting at level 15 you can expend a use of your Action Surge to focus all of your ability into a singular strike. Instead of making multiple attacks, you make a single attack against a target, and if it hits that attack is essentially multiplied by however many attacks you have remaining, including bonuses for each attack from Deft Grip and Flowing Stance; Additionally, after you use this ability, you can make no more attacks on the same turn. This does not prevent a second Action Surge from being used on the same turn. This ability must be declared prior to knowing if the Focused Strike hits.

Omnipath​

At level 18, attunement slots for weapons that require attunement can shared in a single slot however, you can not dual wield weapons that require attunement without using a second slot, you receive no benefit from the weapon when it is not in use (such as auras or other passive buffs), and you can use one of the weapons at a time.

Revised Purple Dragon Knight​

Rallying Cry​

When you choose this archetype at 3rd level, you learn how to inspire your allies to fight on past their injuries.

When you use your Second Wind feature, each creature of your choice within 15 feet of you regains hit points equal to your fighter level, provided that the creature can see or hear you.

Royal Envoy​

A Purple Dragon knight serves as an envoy of the Cormyrean crown. Knights of high standing are expected to conduct themselves with grace.

At 7th level, you gain proficiency in the Persuasion skill. If you are already proficient in it, you gain proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Animal Handling, Insight, Intimidation, or Performance.

Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses Persuasion. You receive this benefit regardless of the skill proficiency you gain from this feature.

Inspiring Surge​

Starting at 10th level, when you use your Action Surge feature, you can choose an amount of creatures equal to half your fighter level (rounded down) within 15 feet of you. Those creatures can make one melee or ranged weapon attack with their reaction, provided that they can see or hear you.

Starting at 18th level, the range of your inspiration increases to 30 feet.

Bulwark​

Beginning at 15th level, you can extend the benefit of your Indomitable feature to your allies. When you decide to use Indomitable to reroll an Intelligence, a Wisdom, or a Charisma saving throw and you aren't incapacitated, you can choose an amount of creatures equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) within 30 feet of you that also failed its saving throw against the same effect. If those creatures can see or hear you, they can reroll their saving throw and must use the new roll.

Loyalty to the Crown​

Starting at 18th level, when you target an ally with any of your bannerette features, that ally gains advantage on the next attack roll, ability check, or saving throw it makes until the end of its next turn.

Jade Lord​

Throughout the continent of kara-tur, one mineral stands above all else in terms of status. Where in the west you would display decadence with gold, in the east, the truest symbol of power is jade. Legends tell of warriors embellished with regality, often proud in status and seeped in regality. The Jade Lords of Shou Lung were legendary figures that displayed power and wealth in equal measure, fighting with both honor and flair.

Regal Countenance​

Even at 3rd level, you carry yourself with grace and nobility. You gain proficiency in either Persuasion or Intimidation. If you already have both of those proficiencies, you can choose a different proficiency instead.

Jade Shards​

At 3rd level, the gutteral flow of combat splinters jade around you. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack or a creature hits you with an attack, a jade shard splinters off of you and orbits you for the duration. You can only have a number of jade shards equal to half your fighter level (rounded up) at a time. When initiative ends, any remaining jade shards drop to the floor and disappear.

You can expend any number of these shards to take special actions called jade tricks. If the trick requires the target to make a saving throw, the DC is calculated as follows:

Jade Trick DC

= 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier (your choice)

If you’re able to make multiple attacks with the Attack action, a trick replaces one of them. You can only use a trick once per attack action.

Jade Blade.​

Make a melee attack with a melee weapon you are holding against a target within range. On a hit, the target suffers the weapon attack’s normal effects and takes a number of d6s in additional damage equal to the amount of shards spent.

Nephrite Burst.​

Make a ranged or thrown weapon attack (your choice) against a target within 120 feet of you. The attack deals a number of d8s of piercing damage equal to the amount of shards spent.

Jadeite Blast.​

Choose a point you can see within 60 feet of you. Creatures within 10 feet of that point must make a Dexterity saving throw or take a number of d10s in slashing damage equal to the jade shards spent.

Mystic Stone​

At 7th level, the jade you create is fortified. Damage from jade tricks count as magical for the purposes of overcoming resistance and immunity to non-magical damage.

Jade Armor​

At 10th level, you learn to pull in the jade shards orbiting you to use as armor. As a bonus action, you can expend any number of jade shards to create jade armor. While this armor is active, you have half cover, or 3/4ths cover if you spent five or more shards. This armor lasts for a number of rounds equal to the jade shards spent or until the end of initiative.

Verdant Generosity​

At 15th level, you are able to protect allies with your jade armor as well. If any ally is attacked or is forced to roll a saving throw, you can use your reaction to expend any amount of jade shards and encase them in jade armor. This armor confers the same amount of cover as your jade armor feature, but only lasts until the end of that ally’s next turn.

Jade Emperor​

At 18th level, you learn a new jade trick.

Jadestorm.​

By expending any amount of jade shards, you can create a jadestorm around you in a 20 foot radius, designating any creatures you wish to be unaffected by it. The storm travels with you, with its area being lightly obscured. An affected creature that begins its turn within the jadestorm takes bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage equal to the shards expended. Additionally, their AC is reduced by half the expended amount (rounded down) until the start of their next turn or until they leave the jadestorm. The jadestorm lasts for the expended amount of rounds or until the end of initiative.

Death Knight​

The archetypal death knight focuses on honing negative energies into their attacks and integrating necromancy into their fighting style. While death knights don’t have to be tragic and dark, the energies used often corrupts the user

Death Tokens​

During combat, when you kill a hostile creature, you gain a number of death tokens equal to half the creature's proficiency bonus (rounded down), which can be used to fuel your death knight abilities. Once a creature grants a death token, it may not do so until you finish a long rest. You may have a maximum number of death tokens equal to 1 + half your fighter level, rounded down, which last until your next long rest.

Undead Soldier​

Starting at 3rd level, you learn to reanimate undead in the midst of combat. As an action, you may expend 2 death tokens to infuse negative energy into a corpse or pile of bones that you can see to turn it into an undead soldier. The corpse becomes a zombie or skeleton which lasts until your next long rest, where it then turns into dust. Undead soldiers share initiative with each other, but roll initiative on their own. As a bonus action, you may give them commands. If no commands are given, they do their best to defend themselves and you. Your undead soldiers additionally count as you for the purpose of gaining death tokens.

You may have a total number of undead soldiers equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down).

Rebuke of the Damned​

Starting at 3rd level, you can expend one death token as a reaction when you or an undead soldier within 30ft of you is hit by an attack. Roll a number of d4 dice equal to your proficiency bonus, and reduce the damage taken by the total rolled, dealing the total back to the attacker as necrotic damage.

Sink or Swim​

Starting at 7th level, you learn various uses for your death tokens.

  • As a bonus action, you can use a death token and select an undead soldier within 30 feet of you. Then, spend and roll up to a number of hit dice equal to your proficiency bonus. The undead soldier regains hit points equal to the total of the dice rolled.
  • As an action, you can spend a number of death tokens and target a undead soldier or corpse within 30 feet of you. Every creature within 10 feet of the undead soldier or corpse must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or take 1d4 necrotic damage and 1d4 fire damage per death token expended.
  • Once per turn, you may expend a death token slay an undead soldier early, instantly turning it into dust. When doing so, you may make an additional attack before the end of your next turn when you take the attack action.

Cull the Horde​

Starting at 10th level, you can use death tokens to empower you and your undead.

  • Your undead soldiers gain a bonus to attack rolls and saving throws equal to half the death tokens you currently carry, rounded down.
  • Attacks done by your undead soldiers count as magical for the purpose of overcoming defenses.
  • You may spend two death tokens when creating an undead soldier to grant the soldier proficiency in light, medium, and heavy armor, shields, and martial weapons.

Black Speech​

Starting at 15th level, You learn to speak, read, and write Black Speech, which can be used to communicate with undead and can not be learned through traditional means. Most undead lack the intelligence to convey or understand sophisticated concepts, but a non-hostile undead could relay what it has seen or heard in the recent past. This ability doesn't grant you friendship with undead, though you can combine this ability with gifts to curry favor with them as you would with any nonplayer character.

Additionally, having access to this speech grants you resistance to necrotic damage.

Grave Warden​

Starting at 18th levels, when using your undead soldier feature, you can expend 6 death tokens to create a sword wraith warrior instead. In addition, you can use death tokens to do the following:

  • Armor of Bones: You may spend a death token when creating an undead to create large spikes of bones which protrude out the created undead. When a creature attacks that undead with a melee attack, they take magical piercing damage equal to your constitution modifier.
  • Lament of the Lost: As a bonus action, you can spend a death token to reposition any of your undead soldiers within 30 feet of you. You determine the new arrangement of these soldiers, and may swap your location with one of them.
