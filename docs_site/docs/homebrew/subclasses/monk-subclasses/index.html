Monk Subclasses

Way of the Feral Fang​

Monks who follow the Way of the Feral Fang tap into their primal instincts, channeling the savage power of beasts. They unleash their inner ferocity, embodying the untamed fury of nature itself.

Feral Strikes​

Starting at 3rd level, when you use your Flurry of Blows feature, you can replace one of the unarmed strikes with the primal savagery cantrip. The primal savagery cantrip uses wisdom as your spellcasting ability modifier for its attack rolls.

Closer to Nature​

Starting at 3rd level, you can choose to learn the druidcraft or shillelagh cantrip, you use wisdom as your spellcasting ability modifier for this cantrip

Bestial Agility​

At 6th level, your connection to primal instincts enhances your speed and agility. You gain the following benefits:

  • You have advantage on saving throws against effects that would cause you to be restrained or paralyzed
  • If you use your Primal savagery cantrip granted by Feral Strikes in a turn you can use your reaction when an enemy makes an attack of opportunity against you to roll a Martial Art Die and add the result to your AC until the end of your turn.

Fury of the Beast​

At 11th level, your strikes become even more savage and relentless. When you hit a creature with your primal savagery cantrip granted by your Feral Strikes feature, you can add your wisdom modifier to the damage dealt, in addition, if you spend 1 ki point, the target must also make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier) or be frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

Primal Resilience​

Starting at 17th level, your primal connection enhances your body’s resilience and ferocity. As a bonus action, you can spend 4 ki points to enter a primal state of feral resilience that last for 1 minute. You gain the following benefits when in this state:

  • You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened.
  • You add an additional Martial Art die to your unarmed strikes
  • You have advantage on all Strength and Dexterity saving throws, and you gain temporary hit points equal to your Monk level at the start of each of your turns. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Way of the Mirror Soul​

Light Echo’s​

3rd-level Mirror Soul feature

Starting when you choose this tradition at 3rd level, you can create reflections that disorient your foes and bolster your defense. When you use your flurry of blows, you can spend 2 ki points to cast the mirror image spell, replacing one of the unarmed strikes. When casted this way, you only make one illusory duplicate, unless you spend one ki point for an additional duplicate (3 duplicates max). For each duplicate you create, you gain 5 temporary hit points.

Fractal Vision​

6th-level Mirror Soul feature

As an action you may magically project your consciousness into a reflective surface that you can see. Projecting allows you to see and hear, as well as project your voice as though you were in that reflections space. During this time, you are blinded and deafened. You can sustain this effect for up to 10 minutes, and you can end it at any time (requires no action).

While being used in this way, you can use an action to project to another reflective surface that you can see. While you are projecting into a reflection, your reflection is visible, and can be interacted with by others, such as speaking with them. If any reflective surface along this path is destroyed or disrupted (in the case of liquids), this effect ends.

Mirror Stride​

11th-level Mirror Soul feature

You gain the ability to magically enter a mirror or reflective surface within 5 feet of you and move from inside it to inside another mirror or reflective surface being effected by your Fractal Vision feature.

You must spend 1 ki point and use 5 feet of movement to enter a reflection. As part of the movement used to enter the reflection, you can either pass into one of those reflections or step out of the reflection you're in. You appear in a spot of your choice within 5 feet of the destination, using another 5 feet of movement. If you have no movement left, you appear within 5 feet of the reflection you entered.

You can use this ability once per round, and take 1d10 force damage if you end your turn inside a reflection.

Soul of Reflection​

17th-level Mirror Soul feature

Starting at 17th level, you empower your Mirror Images for both defense and offense. While your Mirror Images persist, you gain the following benefits:

You and your illusory duplicates gain a +1 to Armor Class and Saving Throws for each duplicate still active (up to a maximum of +3)

Your unarmed strikes and monk weapon attacks deal additional psychic damage equal to your Wisdom modifier. (edited)

Way of the Ki Blade​

Masters of a martial discipline intertwined with the ethereal energies of ki, the practitioners of the Way of the Ki Blade are formidable warriors who wield weapons forged from the very essence of their inner strength. These adept martial artists combine their physical prowess with the disciplined art of ki manipulation, creating a dance of blades that transcends the limits of the mundane.

Ki-Forged Weapon​

At 3rd level, you can spend 1 ki point as a bonus action to manifest a ki-forged weapon, turning a melee monk weapon into a weapon of pure energy for 1 minute. This weapon deals force damage instead of its regular damage type and uses your Wisdom modifier for attack and damage rolls.

In addition, at level 5 the weapon gains a +1 bonus to its attack and damage rolls, unless it is a magic weapon that already has a bonus to those rolls, this bonus increases to +2 at level 11

Disruptive Weapon​

At 6th level, once per turn you can spend a ki-point to temporarily transform your ki-forged weapon into an ethereal weapon. The next melee attack you make with it before the end of your turn ignores the target’s armor, requiring no attack roll. Instead, the target makes a Dexterity saving throw against your Ki save DC. On a failed save, the creature takes damage as if hit by an attack from your ki-forged blade and it's inner balance and movement is disrupted by your ki, the target's speed is halved, and it can't use reactions. On its next turn, it can use either an action or a bonus action, not both. On a successful save, the  target takes half damage from the attack and isn't disrupted. The blade returns to normal immediately after the attack

Ki-Bound Weapon​

At 6th level, If your Ki-Forged blade has the thrown property you can make it return to your hand immediately after it is thrown by spending 1 ki point.

Ki-Enhanced Focus​

At 11th level, your focused ki protects you from mental intrusions and damage. Your thoughts can't be read by telepathy or other means unless you allow it, and you also have resistance to psychic damage.

Ki-Forged Flurry​

At 11th level, when you use your flurry of blows, you can attack with your ki-forged blade instead of unarmed strikes.

Ki-Blade Ascendancy​

At 17th level, your connection to the essence of ki reaches its pinnacle. You can now infuse your ki-forged weapon with extraordinary power. As an action, you can spend 4 ki points to enter the state of Ki-Blade Ascendancy, which lasts for 1 minute.

While in this state:

  • Your ki-forged weapon deals an additional 1d8 force damage on each hit, and you gain a number of temporary hit points equal to twice the force damage dealt by this feature.
  • Whenever you hit a stunned creature with your ki-forged weapon, you can spend an additional ki point to attempt to paralyze the target. The target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your Ki save DC or be paralyzed until the end of its next turn.

Way of the Dragon Turtle​

Curiously, the Way of the Dragon Turtle is a bit of a misnomer for the monks that follow this style. Their link to the legendary beasts is practically nonexistent, only dubbed this way by others for their fortitude and control over heat and steam. Monks that follow this martial style are rarely taught formally, though they may occasionally take on no more than a few students who wish to learn the style. Those who follow the Way of the Dragon Turtle are self-made and remarkable, often gaining an innate understanding of the ki flowing through and around them after overcoming intense hardship. Although this style may seem less refined than its contemporaries, the technical ability of these monks is fatal to underestimate. They pose as much of a threat to their own bodies by manipulating pressure and heat as they do to others, and they must always understand their limits above all else.

Ki-Scorched Body​

Perseverance manifested in the form of raw ki has scorched and hardened your soul and body. When calculating your Monk unarmored defense, you may instead use 13 + your Constitution modifier.

Additionally, your body is altered by the unrefined, explosive ki you harness, often leaving a strange mark on your likeness. You determine its appearance, or generate it randomly by rolling on the Ki-Scorched Characteristic table.

d6Ki-Scorched Characteristic
1Your hands and feet are blackened, giving them the appearance of charcoal.
2Steam often rises from your ears, mouth and body when you experience intense emotions.
3The skin around your elbows and shoulders is cracked and ossified, peeling slightly upward like the scutes of a turtle.
4You have a large scar or burn mark on your body, which becomes searing hot in a fight.
5Still sources of waters nearby you tend to become restless, showing tiny ripples and tremors.
6Your skin fluctuates between feeling extremely hot and cold to the touch.

Impact Combustion​

Your rudimentary abilities in channeling ki allow you to compress and expel moisture and heat. As an action, you can cast fog cloud without providing material components. Once you cast fog cloud using this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a short or long rest, unless you spend 2 ki points to cast it again.

Additionally, you gain the ability to convert the heat and force from blows you take into powerful outbursts. As a bonus action, if you have taken damage between the end of your last turn and the start of your current turn, you may spend 1 ki point to enter the Combustion state until the end of your turn. While in the Combustion state, you gain the following benefits:

  • You make Strength-based attack rolls with advantage.
  • Your unarmed strikes deal additional fire damage on a hit equal to one roll of your Martial Arts die plus your Wisdom modifier.
  • You can make one additional unarmed strike as part of your Attack action.

Pneumatic Blitz​

At 6th level, you gain the ability to catalyze the heat and momentum you build by moving into a volatile enhancement. If you move at least 30 feet in a straight line on your turn, you may immediately spend 2 ki points to increase your walking speed by 30 feet until the end of your turn. If you continue the current movement for at least 60 feet in a straight line, you may choose to emit a blast of excess pressure around you. When you end this movement, each creature within a 10-foot radius of you must succeed on a Strength saving throw or take a number of d10s equal to your proficiency bonus in force damage and be knocked prone. If a creature passes this saving throw, it takes half damage and is not knocked prone.

Body of Vapor​

At 11th level, your control of ki becomes more refined, allowing you to manipulate the elements of your physical form. As an action, you can spend 4 ki points to cast gaseous form on yourself, without providing material components. While in this form, your movement speed is not restricted to 10 feet.

Additionally, you may exit this form at any time on your turn. When you exit this form, you may heal 5d8 hit points. Once you regain hit points using this ability, you can't do so again until you finish a short or long rest.

Pressurized Retribution​

At 17th level, your mastery over the convergence and direction of ki reaches explosive heights. As a reaction to taking damage from a source other than yourself, you may spend up to six ki points. For each ki point you spend as a part of this reaction, you mitigate 5 damage. This damage mitigation is applied before any resistances you have. You may also redirect the force from this mitigated damage, blasting out a 40-foot cone of crushing steam. Creatures in this area must pass a Constitution saving throw against your ki save DC or take 10 fire damage for each ki point you spent as part of this reaction, taking half damage if they pass. Being underwater does not grant resistance to this attack.

Additionally, you are no longer required to have taken damage to enter the Combustion state as a bonus action.
