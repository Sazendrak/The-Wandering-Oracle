Grave Conclave Ranger

Description​

Grave Wardens are grim trackers who walk the line between life and death. They hunt those who would unnaturally subvert the mortal cycle—powerful undead, necromancers, and entities that cling to a profane existence. By drawing on the cold, final energy of the grave, they imbue their weapons with grim power and attune their senses to the echoes of mortality.

Features:

  • 3rd Level - Grave Warden Magic
  • 3rd level - Armament of the Grave
  • 3rd level - Grim Hunter
  • 7th Level - Armament's Vigil
  • 11th Level - Soul's Vigil
  • 15th Level - Veil Walker

3rd Level - Grave Warden Magic​

You learn an additional spell when you reach certain levels in this class, as shown in the Grave Warden Spells table. The spell counts as a ranger spell for you, but it doesn't count against the number of ranger s1pells you know.

Grave Warden Spells​

Ranger Level | Spell

  • 3rd | Inflict Wounds
  • 5th | Ray of Enfeeblement
  • 9th | Speak with Dead
  • 13th | Blight
  • 17th | Negative Energy Flood

3rd level - Armament of the Grave​

You learn a ritual that channels grim power into one weapon. Over 1 hour, which can be done during a short or long rest, you can touch one weapon you are proficient with. This weapon becomes your Bound Armament and gains the following benefits:

  • The weapon counts as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.
  • The weapon can be used as a spellcasting focus for your ranger spells
  • Once on each of your turns, when you hit a creature with an attack using this weapon, the attack deals an extra 1d6 necrotic damage. This extra damage increases to 2d6 at 11th level.

You can perform this ritual again to bond with a different weapon, which ends the bond on the previous one.

3rd level - Grim Hunter​

Your attunement to the echoes of death sharpens your senses. You gain proficiency in your choice of either the Insight or Investigation skill. Additionally, you have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track Undead and Fiends, and on Wisdom (Insight) checks to discern the motives of those creature types.

7th Level - Armament's Vigil​

Your Bound Armament glows faintly, visible only to you, whenever one or more Undead are within 120 feet of you.

Additionally, as a bonus action, or as a reaction when this glow first activates, you can project a 15-foot radius aura of censure. The aura lasts for 1 minute. When an Undead of your choice starts its turn within the aura, it must make a Wisdom saving throw against your ranger spell save DC. On a failed save, it has disadvantage on all attack rolls until the start of its next turn.

You can use this aura a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (a minimum of one). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

11th Level - Soul's Vigil​

Your constant exposure to the energies of undeath fortifies your mind and body. You gain the following benefits:

  • You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed.
  • You gain resistance to necrotic damage.
  • When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you drops to 0 hit points, you can use your reaction to gain temporary hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier + your Ranger level.

15th Level - Veil Walker​

You can step through the veil between life and death. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

  • Grave Step. As a bonus action, you can expend one use of this feature to teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. When you arrive, each creature within 5 feet of you (other than yourself) must make a Dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC. A creature takes *3d10* necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
  • Ethereal Reprisal. As a reaction when you are hit by an attack, you can expend one use of this feature to teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. If this movement takes you out of the attack's range, the attack misses. The creature that attacked you must then make a Dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC, taking *3d10* necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
