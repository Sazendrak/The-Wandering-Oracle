College of Fables

Description​

Any bard can write a story or compose an epic, yet none with as much flair and verbal panache as one who studies under the college of fables. Such bards are masters at crafting narratives, telling tales, and captivating readers far and wide. They tell stories of that which was, that which is, and with enough creative magic, that which will be.

Features:

  • Level 3 - Natural Writer
  • Level 3 - Wordcraft
  • Level 6 - Life Imitates Art
  • Level 14 - Art Imitates Life

Level 3 - Natural Writer​

Beginning at 3rd level, you can spend at least one workweek of downtime (max. 5) and 10 gp of materials per week to write a story. When the story is complete, roll a d20 and add your charisma modifier, as well as a number of dice equal to the amount of workweeks you spent on the story to the roll. The value of the dice is the same as your bardic inspiration dice. The quality of the story you create depends on the result of the roll as shown below.

Story Quality:

Check Totals > Quality

  • 1-10 > Poor
  • 11-20 > Good
  • 21-30 > Great
  • 31-40 > Sensational
  • 41+ > Genre-Definining

Depending on the quality of the book, your reputation as a writer may broaden. Your skills might be recognized by nobles and peasants alike, and you may receive invitations to events that are difficult to enter, and might be able to curry favors with your fame.

Level 3 - Wordcraft​

At 3rd level, when you cast a spell with only verbal components and no somatic or material components, you can expend one use of your bardic inspiration die to cast the spell as a story. When you do so, you may add your bardic inspiration die to one of the spell’s attack rolls, or subtract your bardic inspiration die from the saving throw of one creature affected by the spell. Alternatively, you can have a creature within 60 feet of you that can hear you gain temporary hit points equal to one roll of your bardic inspiration die.

Level 6 - Life Imitates Art​

At 6th level, you can spin together tales that can bend reality around them to an extent. As a reaction to when a creature fails an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw within 60 feet of you, you can describe that course of action succeeding. That creature can then reroll the d20 and use the new roll, potentially allowing it to succeed. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your charisma modifier. You regain any expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Level 14 - Art Imitates Life​

At 14th level, some of your tales are powerful enough to gain corporeal form. As an action, you can create a living story, the manifestation of your wordcraft, in an unoccupied space you can see within range. If you already have a living story, it disappears when the new one is created

The creature is an ally to you and your companions. In combat, the creature shares your initiative count, but it takes its turn immediately after yours. It obeys your verbal commands (no action required by you). If you don’t issue any, it takes the Dodge action and uses its move to avoid danger.

You can only use this feature once per long rest, unless you expend a spell slot of 5th level or higher to use it again.
