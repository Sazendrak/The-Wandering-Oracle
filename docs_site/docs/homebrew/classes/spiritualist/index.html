Spiritualist

Spiritualist​

Spreading a deck of cards out on a table, a fortune teller smiles at her guest, flipping three of the cards over one by one. With a silky voice and a flourished hand, she speaks of events that have transpired, events that weigh heavy on her guest’s mind, and of events yet to come.

Facing down a malevolent spirit, a man in clergical robes watches as it takes control of his companion’s movements. The man thrusts his spirit out towards his ally, wrenching the ghost’s grip away from her mind and allowing her the opportunity to cut the evil being down with a radiant blade.

Standing amidst candles and blood, a medium speaks words into the realm beyond life, listening for a response. The soul of an elder, long deceased, pools into existence next to him, giving him advice and information on how to defeat his greatest foe.

Regardless of the profession they take up, spiritualists utilize the power of their spirit to influence others, both in this world and the next. Whether they specialize in speaking to those long past, hunting down evil wherever it may rear its head, or taking subtle glances into the future to attempt to shape fate, these masters of souls seek to strengthen their ties to the world, both material and ethereal.

Earthly Ambitions​

Spiritualists are masters of their own spirits. Similar to monks that seek spiritual peace, spiritualists spend their lives dedicated to cultivating their souls through exercises and manipulation. Unlike monks, who care little for wealth or power, many spiritualists are driven by earthy goals and ambitions.

Some spiritualists cultivate their abilities to seek vengeance for being wronged, using their spiritual power to further their own agendas. Some have seen a grim future for themselves, and stop at nothing to prevent seeing that future come to pass. Others still are in the profession for material gain, using the opportunities from reading fortunes and hunting evil to fill their coffers with gold. Adventuring is just another means to an end for many of these individuals, often proving to be a quick (yet perilous) path to power.

Mastery Over Spirit​

Constant training and practice allows a spiritualist to use their souls to manipulate the material plane, offering powerful results. The majority of them draw power from the ethereal plane, manipulating the borders between this world and that to gain strange and unconventional abilities. By linking their souls to entities in the material plane, they can influence such entities in a variety of ways, both beneficially and detrimentally. As they learn to control their spirit more effectively, a spiritualist can exploit and influence the world around them in peculiar ways.

Creating a Spiritualist​

As you make a spiritualist, think of what drives you to cultivate your soul and gain power. Are you trying to get revenge on an evil spirit that hurt your friends or family? Do you want to vanquish anything you percieve as evil on your own terms, without the need to be tied down to oath or order? Do you want to make a fortune as a famed seer, that thousands flock towards to find out what lies ahead for their futures? Your motivations may have a big part in deciding the profession you eventually select for yourself.

Besides the things that drive you, it’s also important to decide what extent you’ll go to achieve your ambitions, as well as the methods you might take. Are you willing to make necessary sacrifices to come out ahead? Will you find allies to surround yourself with, and rely on them to assist you with your goals? Maybe you put more of your faith with the dead, and look to them for answers to your problems.

Quick Build​

You can make a spiritualist quickly by following these suggestions. First, Wisdom should be your highest ability score, followed by Dexterity or Constitution. Second, choose the Haunted One background.

Class Features​

As a spiritualist, you gain the following class features.

Hit Points​

Hit Dice:

1d6 per spiritualist level

Hit Points at 1st Level:

6 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels:

1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per spiritualist level after 1st

Proficiencies​

Armor:

Light armor

Weapons:

Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows

Tools:

none

Saving Throws:

Wisdom, Charisma

Skills:

Choose two from Arcana, Deception, Insight, Investigation, Perception, Persuasion, Religion, and Sleight of Hand.

Equipment​

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) a dagger or (b) a quarterstaff
  • (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) a sling with 20 bullets
  • (a) a scholar’s pack or (b) an explorer’s pack
  • leather armor and a spiritual focus

Spirit Tether​

At 1st level, you learn to project your own spirit outwards, allowing it to manipulate the material plane. This tether represents your soul’s influence and will be the baseline for your spiritualist abilities.

Spiritual Focus​

You employ unorthodox tools through which to channel your spirit. You must have a spiritual focus to use your spirit tether, which can take the form of the following objects: a candle, crystal ball, skull, spirit board, or tarokka deck. Alternatively, you may choose a different object you and your DM agree on.

Activating the Spirit Tether​

As a bonus action, you can use your spiritual focus to choose a creature or object other than yourself you can see within 120 feet to latch an invisible spirit rope onto. A creature that can see invisible objects sees a thin, spectral thread directly connecting you to the target. The tether exists in the ethereal plane, and is blocked by any effect that prevents ethereal travel.

You must concentrate to maintain the tether as if concentrating on a spell. The spirit rope breaks if you lose concentration, if you are ever more than 120 feet from the target, if it’s blocked in the ethereal plane, if you create another spirit tether, or if you end the connection as a bonus action.

When you create your tether or as a bonus action on your turn, you can deal 1d4 force damage to the target. The damage increases when you reach certain levels in this class, increasing to 2d4 at 5th level, 3d4 at 11th level, and 4d4 at 17th level.

While you’re tethered to a creature, you can communicate with it telepathically. As long as the creature can understand language, it understands what you communicate even if you don’t share a language and vice versa.

Soul Sense​

Also at 1st level, you have a heightened sensitivity to spirits and souls in your area. As an action, you can sense the general location of any souls within 30 feet of you, including creatures or objects that contain souls. You learn the direction as well as the quantity of the souls you sense, but cannot determine any other properties. This special vision can see through most barriers, but it is blocked by 1 foot of stone, 1 inch of common metal, a thin sheet of lead, or 3 feet of wood or dirt. This effect lasts for one minute and can only be used again after a short or long rest.

Spirit Arts​

At 2nd level, you can manipulate your spirit tether to accomplish feats unrestrained by your mortal body. The extent to which you can manipulate your spirit without exerting yourself is represented by a number of spirit points.

You can spend these spirit points on various spirit arts, which are detailed below. When you spend a spirit point, it is unavailable until you finish a long rest, upon which your spirit is revitalized enough to be utilized further. You can never have more spirit points than shown on the table for your level. Spirit arts can only ever target creatures and objects your spirit tether is connected to. If your spirit tether breaks, the effect of any spirit arts are immediately ended.

Some spirit arts require your target to make a saving throw to resist the art’s effects. The saving throw DC is calculated as follows:

Art save DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Corpus Enforcement​

When your tethered target deals damage to you, you can spend 1 spirit point to halve the damage you take.

Spirit Jolt​

As an action, you can spend up to 3 spirit points (min. 1) and force the target to make a Charisma saving throw. The target takes 3d6 force damage for each point you spend on a failed save, and half as much on a successful one.

Spirit Resonance​

As an action, you can spend 1 spirit point to strengthen the tether between yourself and the tethered target. For one minute, both you and the target can add 1d4 to each saving throw or ability check you make.

Corrupt Spirit​

As an action, you can spend 1 spirit point to corrupt the tethered target. It must make a Charisma saving throw. On a failure, the target must roll a d4 and subtract the amount from each ability check, attack roll, or saving throw it makes until the end of your next turn.

Spirit Influence​

If the tethered target is a creature, you can use an action to spend 1 spirit point and make the target more malleable to influence. For one minute, your checks made against the target have advantage.

Soul Exertion​

At 2nd level, you can exert your spirit past its normal limits with some effort. When you finish a short rest, you can spend any number of hit dice. Rather than regaining hit points, you regain an amount of spirit points equal to the amount you spent.

Spiritual Profession​

At 3rd level, you select a profession to undertake that decides the nature of how you use your spiritual abilities. Your profession grants you features at 3rd level and then again at 6th, 13th, and 18th level.

Ability Score Improvement​

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Otherworldly Tenacity​

At 5th level, if your concentration is broken and you’re not incapacitated, you can spend 1 spirit point to maintain concentration.

Twin Spirit​

At 7th level, when you create a spirit tether, you can target an additional target within range. Whenever you use a spirit art, you can decide if it targets one or both of the targets. If you target multiple creatures or objects with a spirit art, you only have to spend spirit points once. If the tether breaks for a target, only the spirit arts applied to that tether are ended.

Advanced Spirit Arts​

At 9th level, you further hone your spirit arts, allowing you to access more refined techniques. You have access to the following spirit arts:

Soul Blight​

If your tethered target is a creature, you can use an action and spend 2 spirit points to attempt to taint them. The target must make a constitution saving throw or take 5d6 poison damage at the start of each of their turns. The creature can use an action to purge the blight from their body, ending this spirit art.

Tether Swap​

As an action, you can spend 2 spirit points to force the target to make a Charisma save (the target may choose to fail). On a failure, you switch places with the target. If the target is too large to fit in your space or vice versa, the art fails and your spirit points remain unspent.

Panic​

As an action, you can spend 2 spirit points to force the target to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the target is frightened. While frightened, the target must use all of its movement at the start of its turn to move away from you unless there is nowhere else to move. The target can make a Wisdom saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending this spirit art on a success.

Improved Spirit Jolt​

At 10th level, your spirit jolt strengthens in power. Its damage increases to 4d6 force damage for each point you spend.

Heightened Spirit Arts​

Once per turn starting at 11th level, when you use a spirit art that takes an action, you can immediately use a different spirit art that also requires an action. You expend spirit points from both arts.

Master Spirit Arts​

At 14th level, you master control over your spirit, culminating in powerful spirit arts.

Spirit Beyond Form​

If the tethered target would reduce you to 0 hit points, you can spend 3 spirit points as a reaction to drop to 1 hit point instead.

Soul Surge​

If your tethered target is a creature, you can use your action to spend 3 spirit points and strengthen their soul. During their next turn, they may take an extra action. That action can be used only to take the Attack (one weapon attack only), Dash, Disengage, Hide, or Use an Object action.

Oversoul Burst​

If your tethered target is reduced to zero hit points, you can spend 3 spirit points to detonate your soul from its location. Each creature within 10 feet of the target must make a Charisma saving throw. On a failure, the target takes 8d6 force damage, taking half that damage on a success.

Ether Walker​

At 15th level, your spirit can slip into the ethereal without trouble. You can cast the etherealness spell on yourself without expending a spell slot. Once you do so, you cannot do so again until you finish a long rest or spend 5 spirit points to cast it again.

Soul Trinity​

At 17th level, you can split your soul tether even further. You can now target up to three creatures or objects within range when you use your soul tether. Whenever you use a spirit art, you can choose which targets are affected by it.

Soulcast​

At 20th level, you fully master control over your spirit. When you use your soul tether, you can cast your soul out to instantaneously tether to any willing creature on the same plane as yourself. When using your tether in this way, it doesn’t break as a result of being out of range. A creature tethered in this manner can use an action to break the tether at will.
