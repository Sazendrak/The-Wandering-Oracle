Psi Tracker

Description​

In the silent reaches of wild places and hidden thresholds of civilization, there lies a path few can follow: that of the mind-hunter. The Psi Tracker merges the instincts of the wilderness stalker with the latent power of psionics, becoming a tracker not just of footprints and scent, but of thoughts, memories, and the flicker of intent. What begins as simple survival becomes something far deeper: the Psi Tracker reads the currents of the mind, discerns the trail left by fears and desires, and strikes when prey least expects that the hunter knows their inner terrain.

Over long hunts and sustained engagements, the Psi Tracker thrives in drawn-out chases where others would rush to violence. They wear down foes by probing their psyche, disrupting their will, and shifting the battlefield of consciousness. In that way, they become the perfect predator for aberrant threats, whispering shadows of alien thought or intruding into minds as subtly as the wind rustles forest leaves. Their power comes not simply from blade or bow, but from the mind’s edge.

Potential playstyle & motivations for a Psi Tracker:​

  • You prefer slow, methodical engagements rather than outright brawls—tracking, disrupting, then striking.
  • You are motivated by uncovering hidden minds, unraveling secrets, and turning that insight into advantage.
  • You wield psionic power as a tool of the hunt: buffing yourself, marking your quarry, and manipulating the mental battlefield before delivering the kill.

Who would thrive playing this subclass:​

  • Players who enjoy a mix of exploration, investigation, and combat—not just shooting arrows but reading intentions.
  • Players drawn to subtle control-effects, debuffs, and layered tactics rather than pure damage output.
  • Players who like characters with a mysterious aura—someone attuned to mental shadows and the unseen.

Ideal character and players:​

  • A ranger whose backstory involves studying strange psychic phenomena, or hunting aberrations that prey on minds.
  • A player comfortable juggling resources (psionic dice) and making choices about when to engage or wear down enemies.
  • A gamer who wants to emphasize role-play of perception, interrogation, and tracking as much as weapon attacks.

Features:

  • 3rd Level - Psionic Power
  • 3rd Level - Psychic Spells
  • 7th Level - Empathic Awareness
  • 11th Level - Cognitive Bypass
  • 15th level - Mental Dissonance

3rd Level - Psionic Power​

Starting at 3rd level, you harbor a wellspring of psionic energy within yourself. This energy is represented by your Psionic Energy dice, which are each a d6. You have a number of these dice equal to twice your proficiency bonus, and they fuel various psionic powers you have, which are detailed below.

Some of your powers expend the Psionic Energy die they use, as specified in a power's description, and you can't use a power if it requires you to use a die when your dice are all expended. You regain all your expended Psionic Energy dice when you finish a long rest. In addition, as a bonus action, you can regain one expended Psionic Energy die, but you can't do so again until you finish a short or long rest.

When you reach certain levels in this class, the size of your Psionic Energy dice increases: at 5th level (d8), 11th level (d10), and 17th level (d12).

The powers below use your Psionic Energy dice.

Psychic Mark: Whenever you cast Hunters Mark, you can expend a Psionic Energy dice and modify it. When cast in this way, it requires no components and once per turn you can deal additional damage equal to one roll of your Psionic Energy Die when you hit with an attack.

Psionic Fortitude: When your nonpsionic training fails you, your psionic power can help: if you fail a saving throw with which you have proficiency, you can roll one Psionic Energy die, adding half the number rolled to the save, potentially turning failure into success. You expend the die only if the roll succeeds.

3rd Level - Psychic Spells​

When you cast a spell from the Conjuration or Transmutation school, you can do so without Verbal or Somatic components.

7th Level - Empathic Awareness​

Starting at 7th level, your psionics have deepened your awareness of the world around you. You can use your action to roll and expend one Psionic Energy die to open your mind to the emotional and mental resonance of your surroundings. For a number of hours equal to the number rolled, you can sense whether any of the following are present within 3 miles of you (or within 1 mile if you are underground), or have been present within a number of days equal to your Wisdom modifier:

  • Intense thoughts or emotions (fear, anger, grief, ecstasy, anxiety)
  • Presence of Intelligent minds (creatures with Intelligence 6 or higher)
  • Psionic or magical phenomena of significant power (DM’s discretion)

You learn the direction of each detected source within range, but not its exact distance or number.

Additionally, once per use of this feature, you can make a Wisdom (Survival) or Wisdom (Nature) check, with a DC equaling your spell save DC. On a success you learn the distance to one significant location or creature from one of the options above, regardless of distance. If you succeed the check by 5 or more, you learn the distance to a specific location or creature (DM’s discretion), provided you have seen or heard of them before.

Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a short or long rest, unless you expend an additional Psionic Energy die to use it again.

11th Level - Cognitive Bypass​

When you move, you can temporarily blur the distinction between your body and your psychic echo. When you use the Tireless feature, you can roll and expend up to two Psionic Energy dice and add the number rolled to the Temporary Hit Points gained.

Additionally, you become partially incorporeal granting the following benefits for up to 1 minute, or until you are incapacitated:

  • You can move through creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain.
  • The first time you enter a creature's space on a turn, that creature takes force damage equal to one roll of your Psionic Energy dice. Additionally, if you end your turn inside a creature or object, you and that target both take the same amount again in force damage.
  • When you hit a creature you have passed through with a melee weapon attack, you do not provoke opportunity attacks for leaving their reach.

15th level - Mental Dissonance​

Your mastery of psionics lets you unleash waves of psychic intention that collapses thought and will.

Psychic Feedback: Hostile creatures that start their turns within 30ft of you take take Psychic damage equal to your Wisdom modifier if you have attacked or dealt damage to them within the last turn.

Intellect Spike: Additionally, as an action you can target a number of creatures within 30 feet of you equal to half your Wisdom modifier. Creatures targeted in this way must make an Intelligence saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier), taking psychic damage equal to your Wisdom modifier plus two rolls of your Psionic Energy dice and is incapacitated for up to 1 minute. On a successful save they take half damage and their speed is instead halved until the end of their next turn.

At the start of each of their turns while incapacitated, the damage they take from Psychic Feedback is doubled. They can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of their turns, ending on a successful save.

Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest, unless you expend two Psionic Energy dice to use it again.
