Channeler

Channeler​

Clad in rusted armor with a fizzling blade in their right hand, a warrior strikes down their goblin foe with the crackling might of a bolt of lightning. Electricity arcs up and down their blade, converging on the tip and electrocuting its unassuming victim. Laughter echoes across the dank walls of a dungeon as an elf sits, cross-legged, amongst the bodies of her victims, eyes clouded by maddening power. Though her victims show little signs of external damage, their eyes roll up into their sockets, leaving behind only a white gleam. Snowflakes whiz across the air in a beautiful miniature blizzard, centered on a Dragonborn with scales as white as the snow surrounding him. Raising his twin daggers, he weaves together a pattern of rime and frost that leaves his enemies disoriented in a dazzling display of elemental prowess.

These individuals, regardless of origin, share one point in common; pure energy flows through their veins, seemingly bursting from their bodies with little control or conviction. Whether they’ve received this energy through a higher power’s blessing, an experiment gone terribly wrong, or were born as natural capacitors for magic, each of these individuals must quickly learn to channel their internal energy, else it burns them up from the inside.

A Channel for the Arcane Arts​

A channeler is forced to learn to control their energy, as it can be dangerous to both themselves and others to leave it unmanaged and unchecked. How they choose to do so differs from channeler to channeler: some embrace the chaos caused by their energy, some try to hide it and seal it away as much as they can. Some notice the value of the power they hold and try to shape it to their whims, while others still allow their energy to shape themselves and their actions. Regardless of how they decide to use their power, there still comes the issue of channeling it in the first place.Channelers generally train their whole lives to control their energy, from the moment they receive it onwards. Particularly talented channelers might be able to channel their power into objects at will, though regardless of talent, mastery over their power requires hard work. What constitutes ‘mastery’, however, is different from channeler to channeler; some may tell you complete control represents mastery, while another may tell you that you’re not a master lest your mind and body are completely broken under the weight of your own might.

Imbued Weaponry​

Most commonly, channelers use weapons to control their abilities. Channelers use the same martial training these swordsmen and soldiers use to visualize and concentrate their own energy. As martial warriors consider their weapons as extensions of their own body, it’s the easiest for channelers to use weapons as a focus. A trained channeler is as deft with a weapon as any other combatant, and can use their energy to amplify their combat abilities to their liking.

Creating a Channeler​

As you’re making a channeler, you must take a few things into consideration; where did the source of their internal energy come from? Was it the result of a deal with a higher being? A curse put on them by a practiced mage? An artificer’s experiment on humanoid test subjects? These may have all been reasons for the extensive energy you find yourself containing. In addition, think about the method that you used to channel your energy. How you managed to circumvent your energy destroying you internally is just as important as how you managed to receive that energy in the first place.

Class Features​

As a channeler, you gain the following class features.

Hit Points​

Hit Dice: 1d10 per channeler level

Hit Points at 1st Level: 10 + your Constitution Modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d10 (or 6) + your

Constitution modifier per (your class) level after 1st.

Proficiencies​

Armor: Light armor, medium armor

Weapons: Simple weapons, longswords, rapiers

Tools: None

Saving Throws: Constitution, Intelligence

Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Arcana, Athletics,

Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, and

Survival.

Equipment​

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) chain mail and a shield (if proficient) or (b) scale mail or (c) leather armor and 2 daggers
  • (a) a longsword or (b) a rapier or (c) a martial weapon (if proficient)
  • (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) two handaxes
  • (a) a scholar’s pack or (b) an explorer’s pack

Spell Casting Ability​

Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for your channeler spells. The way you channel your innate abilities relies greatly on your experience. You use your Intelligence whenever a spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Intelligence modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a channeler spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one

Spell save DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Spell Attack modifier = your Proficiency Bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Multiclassing​

Ability Score Minimum: STR 13 or DEX 13, and INT 13

Armor: light armor, medium armor

Weapons: simple weapons, longswords, rapiers

Arcane Channel​

Choose one outlet in which to channel your energy. Your choice grants you certain features and proficiencies when you choose it at 1st level. It also grants you additional features at 6th, 11th, and 17th level. The choices are detailed at the bottom of this document.

  • Channel of Artistry
  • Channel of Madness
  • Channel of Volatility
  • Channel of Containment
  • Channel of Devastation

Elemental Imbuement​

Your arcane energy is beginning to leak uncontrollably, forcing you to find a focus to channel it through. Starting at 1st level, whenever you finish a long rest, you can imbue your energy in one weapon you are proficient with. Choose a damage type from acid, fire, cold, lightning, or thunder. The weapon deals an additional 1d4 damage of the chosen type. This damage increases when you reach certain levels in this class: to 1d6 at 10th level, and to 1d8 at 17th level.

When an effect would deal damage using your Elemental Imbuement die, the damage type is the same as your Elemental Imbuement unless stated otherwise.

Arcane Attraction​

At 2nd level, your internal magical power tends to seek out various sources through which to channel itself to, allowing you to get a sense for what is magical and what’s not. As an action, you can sense the presence of magic in the area. Until the end of your next turn, you know the location of each source of magic within 30 feet of you— whether it’s a magic item, a spell, or a creature created by a spell. You know which kind of magic source it is, but you do not know the details of the source; you can’t tell the type of magic item, the specifics of the spell, or the kind of creature.

Once you use this feature, you cannot use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

Fighting Style​

At 2nd level, you adopt a style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one of the following options. You can’t take a Fighting Style option more than once, even if something in the game lets you choose again.

Archery​

You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons.

Dueling.​

When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.

Great Weapon Fighting.​

When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property for you to gain this benefit.

Two-Weapon Fighting.​

When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.

Unarmed Fighting​

Your unarmed strikes can deal bludgeoning damage equal to 1d6 + your Strength modifier on a hit. If you aren’t wielding any weapons or a shield when you make the attack roll, the d6 becomes a d8.

At the start of each of your turns, you can deal 1d4 bludgeoning damage to one creature grappled by you.

Ante​

At 3rd level, your elemental energy burns and crackles within you, proving to be a fickle source of power. You can use the energy to empower yourself, but you run the risk of your power backfiring on you. You gain three Ante options of your choice, detailed at the bottom of this document. You gain another at 7th level, 12th level, and 17th level.

You have an amount of Ante charges equal to your Intelligence modifier. Each use of an Ante consumes a charge. You may not use more than one Ante at the same time. You replenish Ante charges on a short or long rest.

Additionally, when you gain a level in this class, you may replace one Ante option with another that you meet the prerequisites for. A level prerequisite refers to your level in this class.

Ability score improvement​

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Overchanneled Imbuement​

Starting at 5th level, you gain more control over the energy channeled through your weapon of choice. Whenever you finish a long rest, choose two cantrips that share a damage type with your chosen Elemental Imbuement and have a casting time of one action or bonus action. Immediately after you hit with an attack from your imbued weapon, you may cast one of the chosen cantrips as a bonus action. If a spell allows you to choose the damage type you deal, the chosen type must match your Elemental Imbuement.

At 9th level, you may choose two 1st level spells that share a damage type with your chosen Elemental Imbuement. You may cast either of those spells rather than a cantrip without expending a spell slot.

Elemental Boon​

Starting at 7th level, your experience with channeling your energy through weapons is beginning to allow you to channel your energy through living beings as well. As a reaction whenever another ally within 30 feet hits with an attack roll, you can channel your energy through their attack. The attack deals bonus damage equal to your Elemental Imbuement die, and shares the damage type you’d chosen for your Elemental Imbuement.

You may use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier, and you regain all expended uses on a short or long rest.

Raze​

Starting at 10th level, your imbued weapons are growing in destructive force, letting you use them to tear objects to shreds. Attacks made using your imbued weapons deal twice as much damage to objects and structures.

Up The Ante​

Starting at 13th level, the power of your Antes grows, but so do their destructive effects. When you use an Ante, you may choose to Up The Ante, making you roll your Elemental Imbuement die an extra time, adding it to the effect. If the Ante cares about whether the total is even or odd, rather than adding the die an extra time, you instead may use either roll.

Once you use this feature, you must finish a short or long rest before you can use it again.

Flash Charge​

At 14th level, you’ve learned to quickly and deftly control the elemental energies inside of you. Once per day, as an action, you may change the damage type of your Elemental Imbuement, as well as the spells you’d chosen for your Overchanneled Imbuement. You may optionally also choose to imbue your energy in a different weapon.

Greater Boon​

At 15th level, the boons you bestow upon others strengthen to their peak. The elemental boon now deals extra damage equal to twice your Elemental Imbuement die, and the attack ignores damage resistances.

Ruin​

At 18th level, your magic energies can bring down the very foundations of structures around you. By spending one minute concentrating as if concentrating on a spell, you can plunge your imbued weapon into the ground and begin breaking the foundations of nearby buildings.

Once one minute has passed, choose a non-magical object or structure within 60 feet. If that structure can fit within a 30 foot cube, it is immediately destroyed, crumbling to dust. If it is any larger, you can instead destroy a 30 foot cube portion of it within range.

Pinnacle Channel​

At 20th level, you’ve reached the height of elemental control, and can display your mastery on a consistent basis. At the end of a long rest, choose two 2nd or 3rd level spells that share a damage type with your Elemental Imbuement; you may cast those spells at will or any time you can cast one of your Overchanneled Imbuements.

If you use your Flash Charge feature, you may also exchange these spells for any others that fit the prerequisites. Additionally, you may upcast your 1st and 2nd level channelers spells to 3rd level.

Antes​

Antes are a core part of a channeler’s toolkit, and are the representation of their capricious magic. Each ante provides some sort of benefit to you that can help you accomplish certain tasks or utilize certain abilities, but every single one runs the risk of backfiring, leading to inconvencience, injury, or even certain death.

A good channeler knows when to use an ante and when not to take needless risks, but each and every channeler would do well to understand that no matter how many steps one might take to mitigate risk, chances of failure are never zero.

Pinpoint Ante​

When you make an attack roll, you may add your Elemental Imbuement die to it. If the attack misses, your AC decreases by an amount equal to the Elemental Imbuement die’s result until the end of your next turn.

Reflex Ante​

When you make a dexterity saving throw, you may add your Elemental Imbuement die to the save. If the save fails, you take damage equal to twice the Elemental Imbuement die’s result.

Prodigious Ante​

When you make a skill check in a skill you’re not proficient in, you may add your Elemental Imbuement die to the check. If the check fails, you subtract the Elemental Imbuement die’s result from all ability checks for the next hour.

Quickstep Ante​

When someone makes an opportunity attack against you, you may add your Elemental Imbuement die to your AC. If the attack hits, your speed is reduced by five times the result until the end of your turn.

Blasting Ante (Prerequisite: Channel of Devastation)​

When you make an attack roll, you may roll your Elemental Imbuement die. If the attack hits, each creature of your choice other than the target within 15 feet takes damage equal to the result. If the attack misses, each creature other than the target within 15 feet of you takes damage equal to the result.

Mindmeld Ante (Prerequisite: Channel of Madness)​

When a creature makes a saving throw against a channeler spell you cast, you may subtract your Elemental Imbuement die from the save. If the save succeeds, you become the spell’s target, and your saving throw against the spell’s effect is reduced by your Elemental Imbuement die’s result. If the spell would require you to concentrate, it doesn’t require concentration when cast against you.

Curtain-raiser Ante (Prerequisite: Channel of Artistry)​

When you roll initiative, you may add your Elemental Imbuement die to the result. If you’re in the top half of initiative, you have advantage on attack rolls against each creature below you in the initiative order until the end of your next turn. If you’re in the bottom half of initiative, each creature above you in initiative has advantage on attack rolls against you until the end of your next turn.

Unstable Ante (Prerequisite: Channel of Volatility)​

Before you make an attack roll, call if the result will be even or odd. If the result matches your guess, add another Elemental Imbuement die to your Volatile Charge feature. If the results don’t match your guess, you take damage equal to one roll of your Elemental Imbuement die.

Fray Ante (Prerequisite: Channel of Containment)​

If you’re within 5 feet of an enemy, roll your Elemental Imbuement die. If the result is even, you regain hit points equal to three times the roll’s result. If the result is odd, your AC decreases by the roll’s result.

Arcane Ante (Prerequisite: 5th Level)​

Whenever you cast a channeler spell, you may use this Ante. If the spell hits or if the target fails the save against it, you may add two rolls of your Elemental Imbuement die to that spell’s damage. If the spell misses or the target passes the save, you take damage equal to one roll of your Elemental Imbuement die.

Reactive Ante (Prerequisite: 5th Level)​

Whenever an attack roll is made against you, you may use this Ante as a reaction. Roll your Elemental Imbuement die; if the result is even, you gain immunity to the triggering attack’s damage until the end of the turn. If the result is odd, you gain vulnerability to the triggering attack’s damage until the end of the turn.

Goading Ante (Prerequisite: 7th Level, Channel of Devastation)​

When a creature rolls a saving throw against your Devastating Presence feature, you may force them to subtract your Elemental Imbuement die from the result. If they succeed, they add the Elemental Imbuement die’s result to their attack and damage rolls that target you until the end of their turn.

Headspin Ante (Prerequisite: 7th Level, Channel of Madness)​

If a creature within 30 feet of you rolls an Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma saving throw with advantage, you may add your Elemental Imbuement die to the result. If they fail the save, you subtract the Elemental Imbuement die’s result from your saving throws for the next minute.

Blitzkrieg Ante (Prerequisite: 7th Level, Channel of Artistry)​

If you make an attack roll against a creature, you may roll your Elemental Imbuement die. If you hit, your speed increases by an amount equal to five times the result until the end of your turn. If you miss, you fall prone and your speed is reduced by an amount equal to five times the result until the end of your turn.

Surge Ante (Prerequisite: 7th Level, Channel of Volatility)​

After you roll on the Volatile Surge table, you may use this ante. Roll your Elemental Imbuement die and call if the result will be odd or even. If you guess correctly, you may use the effects of both the new and the old roll. If you guess incorrectly, you may use neither effect.

Grasp Ante (Prerequisite: 7th Level, Channel of Containment)​

When you make a Strength (athletics) check to grapple a creature, you can add your Elemental Imbuement die to the check’s result. If the creature succeeds against the grapple, you are restrained until the start of your next turn.

Mental Ante (Prerequisite: 11th Level)​

When you make an Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma saving throw, you may reduce the result by your Elemental Imbuement die. If you pass the save, you may add the Elemental Imbuement die’s result to the failed saving throw of a creature of your choice within 60 feet of you.

Proactive Ante (Prerequisite: 11th Level)​

Whenever an attack roll is made against a creature other than you within 60 feet, you may use this Ante as a reaction. Roll your Elemental Imbuement die; if the result is even, they gain resistance to the triggering attack’s damage until the end of the turn. If the result is odd, you take damage equal to the triggering attack’s damage.

Sticky Ante (Prerequisite: 13th Level, Channel of Devastation)​

You may use this Ante at the start of a creature’s turn. If the creature ends their turn within 5 feet of you, they take damage equal to three rolls of your Elemental Imbuement die. If the creature ends their turn more than 5 feet away from you, you take force damage equal to three rolls of your Elemental Imbuement die.

Hivemind Ante (Prerequisite: 13th Level, Channel of Madness)​

When a creature you can see within 60 feet of you makes a saving throw or ability check that they’re not proficient in, if you’re proficient in that saving throw or ability check, you can roll your Elemental Imbuement die and add the result to their roll. If they fail, you take force damage equal to three times the result.

Haphephobic Ante (Prerequisite: 13th Level, Channel of Artistry)​

When you hit with an attack roll, you may roll your Elemental Imbuement die. If the target damages you before the start of your next turn, you take force damage equal to five times the result. If the target does not damage you before the start of your next turn, they take damage equal to five times the result.

Anti-Ante (Prerequisite: 13th Level, Channel of Volatility)​

You may use this ante immediately after using another ante. Roll your Elemental Imbuement die; if the result is even, neither this Ante nor that Ante consumes an Ante charge. If the result is odd, you lose one Ante charge.

Stance Ante (Prerequisite: 13th Level, Channel of Containment)​

When a creature hits you with an attack, roll a concentration check and add your Elemental Imbuement die. If the check succeeds, you gain resistance to the attack’s damage. If the check fails, you gain vulnerability to the attack’s damage.

Gambler’s Ante (Prerequisite: 18th Level)​

When you use your last Ante charge, you may roll your Elemental Imbuement die; If the result is even, you regain an amount of Ante charges equal to the roll. This amount cannot exceed your maximum. If the result is odd, your hit point total is set to 1. You may only use this Ante once per long rest.

God Ante (Prerequisite: 18th Level)​

You may use this Ante as an action. Roll your Elemental Imbuement die; if the result is even, you may immediately cast a ninth level spell with a casting time of one action from any spell list without expending a spell slot. If the result is odd, you die and cannot be revived except by the effects of a wish spell. You may only use this Ante once per long rest.

Warmind Ante (Prerequisite: 20th Level, Channel of Devastation)​

When you make a melee weapon attack, you may roll your Elemental Imbuement die. Up to that many other willing creatures of your choice within 30 feet of you may make one melee weapon attack as a reaction. If any of them miss, each creature takes damage equal to five times the result.

InsanAnte (Prerequisite: 20th Level, Channel of Madness)​

When you cast an enchantment spell on one or more creatures, you may roll your Elemental Imbuement die. If they fail the save, they take psychic damage equal to four times the result, and if any of them pass the save, you take force damage equal to four times the result.

Masterpiece Ante (Prerequisite: 20th Level, Channel of Artistry)​

When you use your Flash Charge feature, roll your Elemental Imbuement die. The next time you cast a channeler spell, if it hits or if the spell’s target fails its save against it, you gain hit points and temporary hit points equal to three times the result. If it doesn’t hit or the target’s save is successful, you take damage equal to three times the result.

Soulshredder Ante (Prerequisite: 20th Level, Channel of Volatility)​

When you use your Culmination of Volatility feature, you may roll your Elemental Imbuement die. If the result is even, rather than dying immediately, you regain half of your maximum hit points and your gear and equipment aren’t destroyed. If the result is odd, your soul is shredded into nonexistence and you cannot be revived by any means, even through divine intervention or a wish spell.

Havoc Ante (Prerequisite: 20th Level, Channel of Containment)​

When you make an unarmed strike against a creature, they must make a strength saving throw against your spell save DC. Roll your Elemental Imbuement die and subtract it from the save. If they fail, they are knocked back a distance equal to ten times the die’s result. If they succeed, you are knocked back a distance equal to ten times the die’s result. The one knocked back by this effect takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet they traveled.
