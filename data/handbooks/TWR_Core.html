Character Creation

The rules for Character Creation are below. For how to manage characters as they level up, see the Character Progression page.

2014 vs 2024

2014 characters follow the rules in this document as written. 2024 characters, or mixing the two, follow an additional set of rules: Allowed 2024 Rules Content

Character Creation

  1. Unless otherwise stated, rules regarding characters are RAW (rule as written).
    • Approved/Disapproved Modules can be found here: Approved and Disapproved Modules
  2. Characters can start at level 1-3 (player choice)
  3. Items, Experience, Abilities, etc do not carry over from other games/servers/campaigns.
  4. Backgrounds which grant feats are disallowed.
  5. You may choose Fixed or Rolled HP for your character, but must continue with that method for the lifetime of your PC. If rolling, ping a @Dungeonmaster to record your rolls.
  6. Character level advancement is via XP, not Milestone.
  7. All character stats are Point Buy or Standard Array.
  8. Character sheets must be kept up to date. If there is ever a question as to what level you are supposed to be, what items you have, how much gold you have, then we need to be able to refer to your sheet and check it against what we have on record in #records, #story-log or #transaction-log.
  9. You may have no more than 8 player characters to begin with. Once a character reaches T3, you may submit a request for additional PC slots.

Starting Equipment

  1. New PCs begin with standard starting equipment per the PHB.
  2. Starting gold is not allowed.
  3. PCs may select a single Uncommon magic item to start with, if they submit a written backstory that includes receiving it.
    1. This item must be available from the Favor Tables. This can be access in discord with <!loot uncommon wondrous s>, <!loot uncommon armaments s> or any +1 weapon or shield.
    2. The base value of the item may not exceed a gold value of 400gp (i.e. no Mithral Plate, firearms, except a pistol or arquebus, etc.)
  4. PCs who visit Elysium and complete an Entry Survey in character will receive 400gp in additional gold from the player-provided Adventurer's Fund. (LINK NEEDED)
Character Sheet Generators

Dndbeyond is recommended for easy character creation, but you may use another system if you wish. (Avrae supports Dndbeyond, Dicecloud, and Gsheet)

For DNDBeyond users, you may join one of the donated campaigns for access to sourcebooks.

Disclosures

  1. DMs have the right to refuse characters a spot in their quests, including those using UA/Homebrew supplements otherwise permitted by the server

Steps to Submitting a New Character

  1. Submit a link to your character sheet using the Character Submission template pinned in #transcript for Staff review and approval. Once approved, it will be posted by a Staff Member to #active-pc-sheet-links at which time your character can officially be played on the server.
  2. While not required, it is highly recommended you set up a "tupperbot" for your character. This allows you to speak "in character" easier when participating in role-play. Refer to Discord Commands for more information on the tools we use.
  3. While not required, if you desire you may introduce your character in #pc-introductions and include an image and short description of them.

Changing your Starting Choices

Sometimes your original concept for a build doesn't exactly work out. We get it. While you are in T1, you can make any changes you wish to your character to bring it in line with what will be most fun for you to play. Once you have made changes, post a summary to #transcript for review and records keeping. Once your PC is in T2, your choices are locked in without following Retraining Rules where they apply.

Regardless of any other changes your PC's name must not change.

Companion Leveling System

Presenting the Companion Leveling System, allowing creatures special to your characters to gain a spotlight of their own, matching the power-scaling of the character themselves. Below details the system and how your companions can gain levels, and which are eligible for use in this system.

Eligible Companions

All subclass companions are eligible for this system. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Beast Master’s Animal Companion
  • The Extraplanar’s Planar Tether
  • Purple Dragon Knight’s Amethyst Dragon
  • Battle Smith’s Steel Defender

Other creatures can be eligible, but must be either permanently available to you, or otherwise not tied to a duration. Examples of this include:

  • Creatures Tamed by your character
  • Artificer’s Homunculus Servant
  • Spirits Summoned by Find Familiar, Find Steed, or similar magic

Hirelings are not eligible for this system.

Starting the Leveling Process

To start the leveling process for a character’s companion, the character must first take the [Boon of Ascension] detailed later in this document. This boon may be taken multiple times, but only once for each 20 class levels a character has.

When the character takes the boon, they may select a single companion that they control and is eligible for this system, and grant that creature a single level in a class of their choosing. The creature then may gain experience through RP as if they were a paragon PC, with the ability to level up all the way to P26. A companion under this system has the following restrictions:

  • The companion must fill an empty PC slot. You cannot add a creature to this system if you do not have an available slot.
  • The companion does not gain a Boon, Blessing, or Feat at P26, but must still meet the requisite xp to enter a new paragon cycle in order to do so.
  • The companion counts as a controlled entity for the purposes of Hunts, Trials, Raids, etc. A DM can rule otherwise for the purpose of quests.
  • The companion may not reach a level higher than the original character.

In return, the companion gains the following benefits:

  • If the companions actions are normally determined by the original character’s actions, bonus actions, or other similar requirements, they no longer need to do so in order to act.
  • The companion rolls its own initiative in combat.
  • The companion retains any benefits that it had as part of the original character’s class features

Converting Your Companions to Playable Characters

This section contains guidelines for converting your companions into player characters.

Hit Points

When your companion becomes a player character through this system, it retains its previous hit point maximum. It then gains hit points equal to the [Hit Points at 1st Level] hit points of the class it gains, and [Hit Points at Higher Levels] as it gains levels.

Proficiencies

Your companion also retains any skill or saving throw proficiencies it had, as well as any bonuses to them that your class features provided.

In addition, your companion gains the skill, saving throw, tool, weapon, and armor proficiencies that would normally be given with their selected class.

Armor and Other Equipment: More bestial creatures can still make use of barding, of which they are always considered proficient. In order to use weapons, a creature must have a reasonable capacity to use it. If a creature is a size other than medium, any weapons and armor it uses must be properly sized for its use, following the same price conversions as barding.

Submission

You may create a character sheet for your companion on any of the available platforms that allow you to do so. Some may be better suited than others for this. When your sheet is completed, you may submit it as normal to #transcript for approval. Please provide the original character’s name, and specify that the sheet is for a companion when you do so.

Death and Retrieval

Death and Retrieval

Death and Retrieval

If you are being revived by a member of your own party during the course of any content, revival is successful with no penalty aside from the typical cost of casting and any damage to your body or belongings.

If you died but your party succeeded, you may skip the below retrieval steps provided that the party members state your corpse was recovered.

Body Retrieval: Hunts

Characters wishing to return to the scene of a failed hunt to recover their fallen allies or missing friends may do so by engaging the creature that killed them, by targeting them in a hunt. Once the enemy has been defeated, make a body retrieval (Investigation) check.

Body Retrieval: Trials

To retrieve lost bodies in Trials of Tempus, a new party must first spawn into the trial per all the usual rules at the same level of the bodies. The players must then fight through the temple to secure the room containing the corpses before recovery is possible. Once the room is secured, make a body retrieval (Investigation) check.

Body Retrieval: Mapped Hunts

Characters wishing to return to the scene of a failed hunt to recover their fallen allies or missing friends may do so by engaging in a random deadly hunt using the applicable alias of the same tier as the dead party. Once the enemy has been defeated, make a body retrieval (Investigation) check.

Body Retrieval: Delves

  1. The Party is banished to a special retrieval zone that acts like a hunting ground
  2. Any Tier party, excluding Paragon, can attempt to retrieve them, but there needs to be In character motivation or reason to look for the party. #leaveanote
  3. The Party will do a random hunt at deadly difficulty for the Party, upon conclusion of the hunt. A single Investigation check per dead body can be made to retrieve the dead party members.
    1. If the party fails the check, they must do another random hunt to repeat the attempt

Body Retrieval: Other

If you die as a part of a quest or roleplay session run by a @Dungeonmaster, who will provide you details about the location and condition of your body, as well as any necessary information regarding retrieval.

Body Retrieval Checks

Only PCs within the same tier as the dead character may participate in body retrieval. Once the enemy has been defeated, make a body retrieval (Investigation) check:

Player-Run NPCs

Player-Run NPCs

Player-ran NPCs

A player may run an NPC with the following caveats. Breaking any requires the permission of a @Dungeonmaster or @Loremaster.

1: The NPC must act according to its character, or alignment.

2: The NPC cannot be a high-standing or of great importance individual.

3: The NPC must be given adequate reason to physically exist in this plane.

4: The NPC cannot grant PC's mechanical benefits of any sort. Items, Crafting, Gifts, all of the like. Only acceptable as shopkeep or storefronts selling items.

----

Rule 1 dictates that monsters must act relevant to lore. To break such action, or to play an outlier, requires permission.

Rule 2 dictates that NPC roleplayed must be of low bearing. Shopkeep, Bartenders, Apprentices and the generic like. A player cannot run any characters which could influence a greater plot that they are not actively running.

Rule 3 dictates that no creature may exist without bearing. A mind flayer would not exist in a town as a vampire lord would not be sleeping in a forest.

Rule 4 dictates that NPC may not grant mechanical benefits. Pure and simple, save shopkeeps selling mundane items at reasonable (per market discretion) prices.

Agriculture System

Agriculture System

How to Start Your Farm

  1. Choose what crops and land you will be using: Select a crop from the list available in the document below. Each crop has a maximum amount that can be planted per acre, a cost per pound, and a base yield stat which determines the multiplier of how much crops you gain at the end of a growing period. The minimum amount of land you must have to use the agriculture system is one acre, and each crop takes 90 days to plant and/or harvest. To use the agriculture system, you must select an amount of acres of land that you own to become farmland.
  2. Determine the Statistics of your Land: Ping Craftmaster in ⁠#crafting-ooc stating where geographically you are planting. You will be told the statistics of the land, including Disease Resistance, Pest Resistance, Ground quality, and weather. Multiply these together with the yield- this is your overall amount of crops gained. These statistics can be increased for the next growing period using roleplay and money investments submitted to the Craftmaster team.
  3. Find Workers: A hireling of any CR is required to work your land. Each hireling can work on 3 acres for the entire 90 days of a growing period. You must pay the deployed rate for the hireling for each day of the growing period. The amount of time and gold it takes to originally till an acre of land to be ready for crops is negligible. Any supplies and resources used in the growing process are included in the hireling’s pay.
  4. Submit a filled in Template to ⁠#crafting-submissions.

Building Rules

Walls and floors are all considered to be 1 feet thick, a 1 foot by 1 foot by 1 foot cube of material is considered to be 1 unit.

How to Build a Structure

Determine the floor plan of your building. For example, you wish to build a 20 by 20 shack made of stone brick, the walls are say 8 feet high and for ease of calculation the roof is flat, there are no extra internal walls and is just open.

If the floor is 2 feet thick, it will be 20 long x 20 wide x 2 thick = 800 units The ceiling is normal (1 feet thick) and is 400 units Walls are 8 feet high and 20 long x 8 high x 1 wide, and you have 4 of them, therefore they equate to 640 units The building is a total of 1840 units, and can now be priced and the time can be found accordingly.

Crafting an Item

Crafting an Item

Crafting Items

Crafter Information

  • Unnamed NPCs can only work on mundane items, and building projects. These types of crafters require a Named NPC or PC to lead the project. Unnamed NPCs working on mundane equipment are required to be paid at least 25 gp a day.
  • Named NPCs originate one of two ways; either through roleplay ran by a Dungeonmaster and approved by a Craftmaster, or when a PC retires. These types of crafters can lead their own projects, and can increase their magical crafting capabilities through means separate of both PCs and Unnamed NPCs.
  • PCs are characters that are actively being controlled by a player. These types of crafters can lead their own projects, make their own decisions (unless given up by the player to a guild), and are limited in what they can craft based on rarity which is determined based on the level of the character.

Magic Item Proficiency Requirements: A character needs to be proficient with the tools needed to craft an item and have access to the appropriate equipment. Everyone who collaborates needs to have the appropriate tool proficiency. You need to make any judgment calls regarding whether a character has the correct equipment. The following provides some examples.

  • Herbalism Kit: Antitoxin, Potion of Healing
  • Leatherworker's Tool: leather Armor, Boots
  • Smith's Tools: Armor, Weapons
  • Weaver's Tools: Cloaks, Robes
  • Tinker's Tools; Wondrous Items

Note: The above are just examples. If you can justify the use of a tool in a craft/project, it would be acceptable for that craft/project.

Magical Crafting Subsection

Any player characters (active or retired) involved with the crafting needs to meet a level requirement to craft items of certain rarities:

  • Level 1-4: Uncommon and Below
  • Level 5-10: Rare
  • Level 11-16: Very Rare
  • level 17 and up: Legendary

Named NPC: To allow for Named NPC crafters to upgrade the rarities they can craft. A specific amount of crafts of a certain rarity must be made (listed below) as well as a Wisdom check (made by PC) following the final craft (DCs also listed below).

  • Common -> Uncommon: 20 Common crafts with DC 15 Wisdom Check
  • Uncommon -> Rare : 40 Uncommon crafts with DC 18 Wisdom Check
  • Rare -> Very Rare: 60 Rare crafts with DC 20 Wisdom Check
  • Very Rare -> Legendary: 80 Very Rare crafts with DC 22 Wisdom Check

Crafting Time Calculation

When calculating time reduction, the order in which a player should calculate is as follows:

  1. Magic Item Sacrifice
  2. Magic Item Adept (MIA)
  3. Master Scrivener (MS)
  4. Workers
  5. Craft Roleplay Reduction

Magic Item Sacrificing

  • You can sacrifice one item per craft.
  • Lowers base time by half the time it takes to craft an item of the rarity of the item sacrificed.
  • Sacrificing an item that would make the craft instant instead results in the craft taking 1 day.

Magic Item Adept

  • An artificer only class feature that reduces both time and cost of crafting common and uncommon items.

Master Scrivener

  • A wizard only class feature that reduces both time and cost of scribing scrolls.

Workers

  • To a max of five; the more people working on a project, the quicker it gets done. To calculate this reduction method, you divide the base time by the amount of workers you have on the project.
  • Players who have not received the @player role cannot assist with a craft as a worker

Craft Roleplay Reduction

  • Add the initial roleplay in the crafting submission.
  • Additional roleplay regarding the craft, brew, or construction can be submitted in the crafting submissions channel.
  • No XP or favor can be given out in the same request as time reduction.
  • The Craftmaster approving the log, and any subsequent RP submissions that may follow, will determine how long it reduces by, to a max of three days per submission. No reduction can take the completion time below 1 day.
  • Reductions are capped at a 25% total reduction to the original crafting time.
  • Each roleplay request can reduce the log by more than three days, but this requires two or more Craftmaster approval.

Consumables take half the time and cost needed, and require 1 less monster material to craft, down to a minimum of 1 material.

Crafting material Harvesting

A player can harvest crafting materials from hunts.

A player can use survival, nature or investigation to harvest material. The DC of the check is based on CR as seen below:

  • CR 1-3: DC 13
  • CR 4-8: DC 17
  • CR 9-13: DC 21
  • CR 14-18: DC 24
  • CR 19+: DC 26

Homebrew Upgrading Rules

You can add a + enchantment onto an existing magic item. This upgrade will boost the crafted item's rarity a tier up, starting at the higher rarity item. The item's rarity can't exceed legendary, and the + enchantment can't exceed +3.

  • Example 1: Adding +1 to a Dragon Slayer Sword will make it Very Rare and +2
  • Example 2: Adding +1 to a Serpent Scale Armor will make it a Very Rare and +1

This upgrading process is limited to weapons and armors only.

Re-forging

You can re-forge the type of item you have according to the class and enchantment of the item.

  • The class of item refers to the grouping allowed by the enchantment, found in parenthesis after the item type in its description. Whether that be simple/martial, melee/ranged, any, any kind of sword, etc.
  • The enchantment of the item refers to the magical ability of the item.

You can only re-forge an item to another item of the same class and enchantment. For example, a Dragon Slayer Longsword couldn't be re-forged into a Flame Tongue Greatsword, but it could be re-forged into a Dragon Slayer Rapier.

Re-forge Cost: Half of the cost it takes to craft an item of that rarity.

Re-forge Time: Half of the time it takes to craft an item of that rarity.

  • Re-forging is only applicable with weapons and armor.
  • Magic Item Adept feature works with re-forging.

Selling Magic Items

Players can now sell magic items to NPCs for gold, or offer it to a deity of their choice for favor.

Selling to NPCs gives the character gold equal to half the minimum price of that item. Offering it up to a deity of their choice gives the character favor equal to a quarter of what it costs to roll a random item of that category.

Selling Mundane Items

There are three item types: Scrap, Old, and New.

  • Scrap sells at 10% of sale value. Unusable
  • Old sells at 50%. Used & Starter Gear
  • New sells at 100%. Recently Forged

Repairing takes 1/8 of the item cost in material, and value/100 days. For example, 15 days for plate, around 200 gp to repair.

If Mending is used, scrap items are repaired into functional old items. Halving time on repair, unless additional replacement material is required.

Monster Parts

Crafting materials are only interchangeable for money. Monster Parts may not be traded for magic items

Gifting Rules

Characters may not gift each other money or items at will. Ventures

Ventures

Business empires and peddler wagons. Player characters can now obtain money, experience, and more by roleplaying their business ventures. The owner of a venture, need not be personally involved. Narration of employees and the setting or providing venues for an event qualifies for gain.

Investment:

The value of things committed to make coin. Capital is the stuff that are not consumed in the venture. General examples: land, property, and facilities.

Gain:

Scaling from 0 to 200% of the investment. Graded by pinks and bookworms.

Because we do not capture depreciation here, more weight will be given to expended coin than capital, which can be reused. Do not be surprised if your million gold cargo ship does not get you 1.5 million in returns.

Distribution:

Player notes the percentage of gain given to people involved.

If you wish for particular items, materials, or other gains that are not coin, do it here and mark to whom it should go to.

Pinks/bookworms will revise this section which tells the player(s) what was earned. Including RPXP.

Cleave

If you hit a creature with a melee attack roll using this weapon, you can make a melee attack roll with the weapon against a second creature within 5 feet of the first that is also within your reach. On a hit, the second creature takes the weapon’s damage, but don’t add your ability modifier to that damage unless that modifier is negative. You can make this extra attack only once per turn.

Graze

If your attack roll with this weapon misses a creature, you can deal damage to that creature equal to the ability modifier you used to make the attack roll. This damage is the same type dealt by the weapon, and the damage can be increased only by increasing the ability modifier.

Nick

When you make the extra attack from two-weapon fighting, you can make it as part of the Attack action instead of as a Bonus Action. You can make this extra attack only once per turn.

Push

If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can push the creature up to 10 feet straight away from yourself if it is Large or smaller.

Sap

If you hit a creature with this weapon, that creature has Disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of your next turn.

Slow

If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage to it, you can reduce its Speed by 10 feet until the start of your next turn. If the creature is hit more than once by weapons that have this property, the Speed reduction doesn’t exceed 10 feet.

Topple

If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force the creature to make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 plus the ability modifier used to make the attack roll and your Proficiency Bonus). On a failed save, the creature has the Prone condition.

Vex

If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage to the creature, you have Advantage on your next attack roll against that creature before the end of your next turn.

Gathering

  1. Use Downtime to Gather Herbs, max usage 3x a day (uses 8 hours in a session). Note: You can no longer sacrifice adventuring time to harvest extra herbs.

  2. Choose one environment [Cave, Forest, Desert, Grasslands, Swamp] + Rarity [Common, Uncommon, Rare, Very Rare]

  3. Roll Nature/Medicine/Arcana + Herbalism/Poisoner/Brewer/Alchemist Tool Prof Bonus (If proficient) --> DC scales depending on rarity of ingredient. Once check is completed, post in transcript using template found in pins of transcript . Craftmaster will roll for the herb gathered randomly depending on location chosen and DC rarity met.

Rarity DC
Common DC 10
Uncommon DC 13
Rare DC 17
Very Rare DC 20
Targeted Gathering

  1. You can target herbs based on which potion you wish to make! If you are targeting a herb for a specific potion, use the Targeted gathering Template pinned in transcript

  2. If the herb you are searching for isn't in the environment you chose, you get nothing. If it is, a Craftmaster will roll the herb normally, and you only get the herb if the dice comply.

The DCs for targeted gathering are as followed:
Rarity DC
Common DC 13
Uncommon DC 17
Rare DC 20
Very Rare DC 23
Bonuses:
  1. You can only target one herb per day until you have gathered 3 of that herb, at which point you may target for two herbs per day due to your familiarity.
  2. You cannot use help actions in herb gathering. Self-Buffs are allowed for now.
  3. Nat20 Rule: If you roll nat20 on the check (RAW Nat20 without modifiers and bonuses) you get 1 Extra Herb.
  4. Over10 Rule: If your total roll is 10 over the applicable DC you get 1 Extra Herb. Example, if your total roll for a rare is 31, you get 2 Herbs instead of 1.
  5. Nat20 and Over10 rules can both apply, to a max of 3 Herbs per roll.

Potion Brewing

Potions are brewed using herbs, each herb is used for a specific potion, the potion is hinted at in the description.

Bulk Brewing

  • Bulk Brewing has a discount on overall cost now. For every 10 potions you make at a time, you get a 5% discount on cost, cap for the discount is 20%.
  • Bulk Brewing also requires less herbs. For every 10 potions you brew, you only need 7x the amount of herbs for one potion of the same rarity. Example: 10 common potions of healing - 7 enchantment herbs needed.

Time Reduction

  • You get a 50% time reduction if you use double the amount of enchantment herbs for the potion.
  • Sacrificing Potions:
  1.   You can sacrifice one potion per brew.
  2.   Lowers base time by half the time it takes to brew a potion of that rarity.

Daily Life on TWR

Daily Life on TWR

The Passage of Time

Time passes at the same rate within the world of TWR that it passes in our world. For easy reference, there is a channel in the server that lists the time on Elysium. This is an optional time you can use for coordination and envinronmental change, but you may act out your character’s life within each day as it meets your schedule.

Daily Reset

For the purposes of downtime and daily activities, your PC’s day begins when you wake up in the morning and ends when you go to bed. For fringe cases, daily resets should occur roughly every 24 hours.

Daily Resources

Each day, characters have the following resources:

Hunting Tags - 5
Trial Tags - 2
Downtime Hours - 8
Long Rest - 1 *
Short Rest - 8 *

Long and Short Rests

Aside from rests which occur in Trials or Quests, players are limited to 1 Long Rest and 8 Short Rests per day. These can be taken at the player’s discretion by using the !sr or !lr command in discord.

Quest Locks & Restrictions

During the course of a quest, characters may be restricted by the DM from pursuing hunts, trials, downtime options and rests as usual. While your PC is attending a quest, work with your DM to determine what parts of their daily routine can be continued.

Repetitive Actions

Some spells or projects require daily activity to fully realize. In cases like this, players should post in #transcript describing the action, how long they will repeat it, and outlining all the materials required (which must be procured ahead of time). Staff will then review the post, notify you of any issues or extra requirements, and when the time has passed your actions will become official.

Downtime

Each PC may utilize 8 hours per day of downtime to further their character’s goals and story. Downtime options can be pursued as outlined by RAW in books such as the PHB, DMG, and XGtE. Some examples include crafting magical items, performing research, gambling, or working a mundane job. Additional downtime options may be defined in other TWR supplements.

This server uses the “Optional Rule” for sleep deprivation located in Xanathar’s Guide to Everything if you wish to work through the night.

Retraining Rules

Retraining Rules

Retraining Rules

Class Retraining

By spending 10 workweeks of downtime, a character can choose to replace one of their levels in one class with a level in another class. This time is reduced by a number of workweeks equal to the character’s ability modifier in the key ability of the new class. If their new class has more than one key ability, the characters lowest ability modifier should be used for this calculation. The character must have a minimum of 13 in the key abilities of both their current class and their new class They must have an instructor of at least 1 level higher (or be level 20) than your new level in the new class and must spend 50gp per week for the instructor and relevant supplies.

Fighting Style Retraining

(For Fighters, Rangers, Paladins, Blood Hunters, and College of Swords Bards only) By spending a week of downtime, you can choose one of the (insert class name here) fighting style you know and replace it with another fighting style from the (insert class name here) class.

Feat / ASI Retraining

By spending a week of downtime, you can choose to replace one Feat or ASI you qualify for with another Feat or ASI you qualify for. Post20 ASIs can be retrained only to ASIs, and Post20 Feats can be retrained only to Boons or Feats.

Spell Retraining

(For sorcerers, warlocks, bards and rangers only) By spending a week of downtime,you can choose one of the (insert class name here) spells you know and replace it with another spell from the (insert class name here) spell list, which also must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

Mystic Arcanum Retraining

Warlocks can spend 1 week of downtime to retrain one of their mystic arcanum spells for another spell that is applicable for that version of the mystic arcanum classfeat.

RP Reduction

When you undergo retraining, you can submit an RP-XP request for roleplay related to the retraining and receive a reduction in the overall time spent retraining. Each RP-XP request is worth a 5% reduction, up to a maximum of 25%.

Soulbound Item

Soulbound Items

In the ancient epochs, when the world was young and the weave of magic was still being shaped, the gods and primordial forces sought to leave their mark upon the mortal realm. They imbued certain objects with Runes of their essence, creating artifacts that resonated with the very souls of those who wielded them. These are the Soulbound Items.

A Soulbound Item is more than just a tool; it is a companion, a reflection of the bond between the bearer and the object. To attune to such an item is to forge a pact, intertwining one's fate with the artifact's power. As the wielder grows in strength and experience, so too does the Soulbound Item, awakening latent abilities and evolving in response to the challenges faced together.

However, this bond is not without its trials. The path to unlocking a Soulbound Item's full potential is fraught with challenges, requiring the bearer to prove their worth and commitment. Only through perseverance, valor, and a deep connection can the true power of the Soulbound Item be realized.

Soulbound Item Mechanics

  1. A Player Character can only ever be attuned to one Soulbound Item
  2. The Item is a relic

Progressing the Soulbound Item

  1. Each Soulbound Item has its progression on the relevant Item Progression table
  2. At certain levels the weapon gains one of 4 types of features, Base, Ability, Unique and Runes. Each feature will have options to choose from.
  3. For Ability and Unique features, The player can decide whether to take a Novice Rank option or to promote an previously chosen option to the next rank
  4. Runes provide minor benefits, where some Runes you need more than 1 before unlocking its benefits
  5. When you choose an option as part of a feature you cannot choose that option again, other to promote it, unless it's stated otherwise.
  6. Ability and Unique feature ranks are constrained limited by the strength of its owner. To unlock an Expert feature the owner must be level 11 or higher, to unlock a Master feature they must be level 17 or higher
  7. The Weapon can at no point be at a higher level than it's owner

Item Leveling

  1. While the item is attuned to a character, any experience that the player gains while attuned to the item is split 50/50 between the player and their soulbound item
    1. The XP will be scaled to the item's level via an alias.
    2. This includes XP from random hunts, dungeons, raids and quests.
    3. It is the players choice if up to 50% of RPXP goes to the weapon
    4. Example:
      1. If a level 8 Player gets 5000XP for Killing a Monster in a hunt and the item is also level 8, the Player and the Item each gets 2500XP
      2. If the weapon was at level 5, Player would get 2500XP which is 18% of XP Required to level to level 9. Thus the Item will get 18% of the exp required to go from level 5 to level 6, which is 1350 XP.
    5. When a player caps their level, the Item will still get 50% of the Exp that the player would have received
    6. This seems like complicated math, but there will be an alias to take care of all that
  2. Paragons:
    1. 50% of the experience gained from non-hunt combat participation can still go towards the weapon, this will be scaled for the Weapons level
    2. Paragon's Soulbound Items receive 50% of the Experience they would have gotten from combat. This includes Non-targetted hunts, Delves, Trials and Raids

Obtaining a Soulbound Item

  1. The secret of creating a soulbound item has been unlocked, but as the magic is tied to the wielder and not the crafting they can only be crafted at its common stage.
  2. In lieu of choosing an uncommon item tied to the backstory of a submitted character, a player can choose a Soulbound* item, with the item starting at the same level of the Character, however this should be covered in the backstory
  3. Soulbound Items can be traded, but they will start at level 1 for a new PC and progress will be reset. For all intents and purposes when being traded, they use common item selling values.

Retraining Item Levels

  1. By spending 1 workweek of downtime a Level feature can be retrained to a different option for that level.
  2. Only a single level can be retrained at a a time
    • If you are Master of item Feature A, and want to retrain the weapon into a Journeyman of Path B and Path A, you will spend 1 workweek to go to Expert Path A and Novice Path B and then another workweek to reach Journeyman Path A and Journeyman Path B

Reset

  1. A soulbound weapon can be reset at anytime. This does mean that that it restarts at level 1, and any progress or experience it gained will be lost.

Homebrewing

  1. Players are Encouraged to Homebrew their own Unique Features and Runes via Homebrew System & Index, this will expand the options for other players as well

Future Updates

  1. As this is a new system, it may receive some balancing updates, either with nerf, buffs or sideways adjustments. Participation in this system acknowledges that your Item may change.

Runes

As the Soulbound Item grows in power, etchings, runes or engraving may appear on it, this will either directly confer benefits, or in combination with other runes provide additional benefits.

Universal Runes:

These Runes can be used on weapon, armor, shields and foci:

  • Reclamation Rune - You can return the item to your hand (or your body in the case of armor) as an action.
  • Quick Reclamation Rune - You can return the item to your hand (or your body in the case of armor) as a bonus action, requires Reclamation Rune
  • Glamour Rune - You can freely change the appearance of the item as an action.
  • Compass Rune - While holding or wearing the item, you can determine the direction of true north, or the direction to a creature of which you speak its name.
  • Echo Rune - You can cast the message cantrip while the item is on your person.
  • Skill Runes - Once the item has accumulated two skill Runes, you gain proficiency in one skill of your choice while attuned to the item. An item can take skill Runes more than two times, and grant a new skill for every two of them it has accumulated.
  • Shifting Rune - Once the item has two shifting Runes, over the course of a long rest, you can move the properties of the item to another of the same category, in your possession, making the new item your soulbound item. For example, you can move the properties of a soulbound dagger onto a mundane spear, receiving a soulbound spear.
  • Shining Rune - The item sheds bright light out 10 feet, and dim light another 10 feet. This Rune can be taken a second time, doubling the radius of the light.
  • Specialization Rune - Once an item has accumulated four specialization Runes, the item gains one level of any path, ward, or conduit.
  • Intricate Rune - Once an item has accumulated four intricate Runes, the item gains one point to spend on any base feature.
  • Mnemonic Rune (2x) – The item can “store” one memory you designate. At 2 Mnemonic Runes, you can replay it vividly.
  • Expertise Runes - Once the item has accumulated two skill Runes, and two expertise Runes, you can add double your proficiency to the skill Rune skill, instead of normal proficiency.
  • Awakening Rune - When you have two Awaking Runes, your weapon gains sentience. See link for detail information Awakening Rune

Weapon Runes:

These Runes can only be taken on weapons:

  • Twin Rune - As a bonus action, you may split your weapon into two identical light versions for two-weapon fighting. They recombine as a bonus action, only works with weapons with the light property. Each of these weapons function as your Soulbound weapon, but still only requires one attunement slot.
  • Master Reclamation Rune - Immediately after hitting or missing, the weapon flies back to your hand, this requires a Quick Reclamation Rune, Thrown Property and a Level 12+ Soulbound weapon
  • Identical Rune - This rune bypasses the light requirement of the twin rune, allowing you split one handed and versatile weapons with the twin rune, that don't have the light property
  • Hunter’s Rune - While attuned, you can spend 1 minute studying a creature’s tracks or remnants to learn the direction it traveled within the past 24 hours.
  • Sanguine Rune - Once per short rest, when you reduce a creature to 0 HP, you get THP equal to your Path Die.
  • Dueling Spirit Runes - With 2 Deuling Spirit Runes, while fighting with the weapon in one hand and no other weapon or shield in the other, you gain +1 AC. With 4 invested, increase it to +2 AC.

Armor/Shield Runes:

These Runes can only be taken on Armor or Shields:

  • Defiant Rune – When you have 2 Defiant Runes, Once per long rest, when reduced to 0 HP while wearing the ward, you drop to 1 HP instead.
  • Aegis Rune – When you have 2 Aegis Runes and you succeed on a saving throw against a spell, you can use your reaction to grant advantage to one ally within 10 feet against the same effect.
  • Mirrorplate Rune – As a reaction once per short rest, impose disadvantage on an attack against you.
  • Beacon Runes – When you have 2 Beacon Runes, allies within 5 feet gain +1 on saving throws against being frightened. At 4 Runes, the aura extends to 10 feet and grants advantage instead.
  • Masterwork Rune - If you have 3 Masterwork Runes, you can upgrade your armor to highest tier armor of its type, any light armor, becomes studded leather, medium armor becomes half-plate and heavy armor becomes plate.

Focus Runes:

These Runes can only be taken on a focus:

Regardless of how many Runes you have, you can only store 1 spell, and you can cast the spell without expending a spell slot.
  • Scryer’s Rune – Once per long rest, you can use the conduit as a divining rod, giving you advantage on a single Wisdom (Perception) or (Survival) check.
  • Resonance Runes – (Requires 2+) When you cast a spell, faint motes of energy linger. Allies within 5 feet gain +1 to their next attack roll. At 4 resonance Runes, the bonus is +1d4 instead.

TWR DM's Guide

What is this Guide?

This page is a guide to getting started running Quests on TWR, a discord-based Westmarches campaign. Quests can be run by any player, with certain exceptions where the player may need to seek approval from staff for lore/balance reasons. As noted in our Code of Conduct, while running a quest or other content, the player acts as the DM and is considered such during play.

Preparing to DM

Being a DM on TWR is easy and fun. Most of the adventures can be prepared in a short period of time, and you don’t have to create an entire setting and form a campaign or spend a ton of time plotting out a large overarching plot. Most Quests on TWR are run as “one-shots,” though more advanced DMs may choose to run quests in a series to tell a more intricate story.

What you Need to Run a Game

In order to run a game as a DM on TWR, you’ll need the following:

Players

The #quest-board channel is the perfect place to seek out players, if you do not have a full group in mind already. For traditional adventures a group of 4-6 players is recommended, but there is no specific number of players required to participate in a quest. Just be sure to balance any potential combat or roleplay encounters around the number of participants to ensure everyone gets to play.

A Plot

Balance the three pillars of gameplay (Roleplay, Adventuring, Combat) to your liking and create a plot for your quest. It is best to keep plots simple at first until you’ve got some practice running Quests. Consider what type of story you want to tell, what type of rewards might fit with the quest, Prepare any puzzles, difficult choices, or monster statblocks for combat ahead of time so that you can spend gameplay time interacting with the players and building the story. Remember, CR values translate to what an average difficulty is for a group of 4 well balanced adventurers. Though combat should be challenging, it should still be possible to win (unless your story dictates that the party loses!).

A Format

Some DMs prefer to run quests in a dedicated session for a more traditional experience, planning a start and end time. Others prefer a play-by-post (PBP) format, which can span multiple days or weeks and provide a more relaxed pace. Each option has benefits and detriments, so it's important to specify which one in your #quest-board post to attract the right players.

Other Tools

Avrae

For combat, spellcasting, and other mechanical functions it is highly recommended that you use Avrae which will automate the majority of tracking and run combat. You can also upload maps to use for tactical placement during combat.

Tupperbot

You will want to set up at least a "Narrator Tupper" to describe scenes, effects and other miscellaneous actions during play. You may also consider creating specific Tuppers for your NPCs to improve immersion and create a more aesthetically pleasing experience.

Group Composition

There’s no general rule that requires members of a group be of the same level. In quests that revolve around combat, we recommend restricting them by Tier as defined in the Player’s Handbook and Rules Compendium. For quests that are more roleplay-centric, this is less necessary, but bear in mind that some high level PCs may have spells and abilities that far outshine the rest of the group.

Facilitator of Fun

You have the most important role. You guide the narrative and provide the players with an immersive and enjoyable quest. While guiding the players, it is also your responsibility to make everyone feel welcomed, creating a fun and fair environment. Here are some things to keep in mind:

You are Empowered

Make decisions about how the group interacts with the quest; adjust or improvise your plan as needed. You have the ability to create NPCs, locations, challenges and scenarios with broad authority within your quest. Interacting with official D&D NPCs, Factions, Deities and other key lore entities may require approval from Staff; we encourage DMs who are concerned that their content may not accurately reflect the setting reach out to an official Dungeonmaster or Loremaster for guidance. See the "When to Seek Staff Approval" section below for a better understanding of when to reach out. Be aware that, in the instance of particularly challenging deviations from the setting Staff may have to retcon the contents of your quests to maintain fairness and server continuity.

Challenge your Players

Gauge the experience level of your players, as well as what they enjoy in a game and attempt to deliver what they’re after; everyone should be able to shine. You can adjust an encounter’s difficulty by adding or removing thematically appropriate monsters but can’t create new monsters or modify them in such a way that modifies their challenge rating (aside from changing their hit points within the range afforded by their hit dice). Be careful of going too far, however. The intent is to have a collaborative and challenging story-telling experience; it’s not to do everything you can to kill your players’ characters. Similarly, destroying equipment generally shouldn’t happen unless an encounter specifically directs you to. In fact, unless they have conditions detailing their destruction (such as an ioun stone), permanent magic items can’t be destroyed.

Dealing with Disruptive Players

If a player is acting in a disruptive manner during a play session, attempt to address the issue as soon as it becomes clear that someone (including you) might be uncomfortable. Take the player aside and explain why the behavior might be causing others to feel uncomfortable. If the problem persists, contact a Moderator or Admin for help with resolution.

Disruptive Magic Items, Spells or Class Features

There are several magic items, spells, class features and homebrew submissions within the rules of TWR that might become disruptive to the overall enjoyment of others at the table. If use of any personal equipment, features etc. through play leads to an uncomfortable play experience, take the player aside and explain the issue. You may wish to discuss temporarily removing the item or ability from the character and providing them an alternative replacement instead. You may choose to review a character’s sheet prior to play to help identify any potential issues, You should not mandate removal of magic items or gear solely on your preferences, but rather on their impact to the story and enjoyment of the players/DM as a group.

Safety Tools

Safety tools help ensure players aren’t pushed beyond their comfort levels. These tools let your players know you want them to have a positive experience. Some common safety tools are provided below, each with a summary of how they work. An article about one such safety kit is provided to help with further information. Ensure that you discuss these tools with your players before the game:

  • Code of Conduct: TWR has a code of conduct that outlines rules for play across the server. It defines desired and prohibited behaviors and possible consequences. Familiarize yourself with the Code of Conduct before running a quest to help understand what is acceptable and what is not.
  • Pregame, Postgame, and Out of Character Discussions: Use time before the game to explain content warnings and set boundaries. This helps you understand the players' various goals and comfort levels. Be sure to discuss the possibility of lethal combat, if applicable, as many players prefer to avoid risking the safety of their long-running PCs. During play, most DMs prefer to have an Out of Character (OOC) thread for combat and ongoing discussion of the story. After the quest, decompress and discuss what worked and what didn't with the players.
  • Be Welcoming: Welcome and encourage respectful and open conversations about issues as they arise. Show that you’re listening by avoiding defensive responses.
  • Direct Communication: Allow direct messaging between the players and yourself as the DM. Generally, do not share information sent to you in private with the rest of the group without permission.

Keep the Game Moving

If the game gets bogged down, provide hints and clues to your players facing puzzles or engaging in combat and roleplay interactions that get frustrating. This gives players “little victories” for making good choices based on clues. Don't be afraid to skip someone's turn or allow them the ability to act retroactively in the future if one player is unavailable and disrupting the flow of play.

Running the Game

As the DM, you are charged with not only guiding the story, but also fairly adjudicating the game’s rules and rewarding your players’ characters for their accomplishments.

The Rules of the Game

TWR generally uses Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition (2014) with some added Homebrew Rules. You can issue rulings to your table when the rules of the game are ambiguous or vague, but you must otherwise adhere to the rules as they are provided in the core rulebooks and can’t change them or make up your own. You must always use the most current incarnation of a rule. Oftentimes, if a rule is in dispute, you can go to the #questions channel or ping a Dungeonmaster for assistance; generally, we follow Sage Advice for clarification from the game developers and, in some cases, Homebrew or #town-hall posts will include rules clarifications. Don't be afraid to ask for help, or issue a temporary ruling while you wait to maintain the flow of play. Otherwise, the rules as written in the rulebooks always take precedence.

Quest Locks

Depending on the content of your quest, you have the ability to "Quest Lock" PCs who are participating. Locked PCs cannot gain experience from or participate in Hunts, Trials, or any form of activity outside of your quest. They are also unable to benefit from downtime while engaged in your quest. Whether or not to lock a PC is up to you, as is whether or not their possible progression applies within your quest. Keep in mind travel, in-character duration, and mechanical balance when deciding whether or not to lock the PCs, and be sure to communicate a quest lock during your original post or pregrame discussion.

Death, Disease and Curses

Adventuring is a risky job, and bad things often happen to adventurers. Diseases, poisons, and other similar effects persist until they’re removed, and death within a quest applies to a PC outside of the quest as well. However, thanks to the proliferation of healing and revival services from players in our setting, they are often simple to overcome. If you wish to enact permanent effects, be sure to communicate that possibility to the players during your original post or pregame discussion.

  • Providing players with information relevant to ongoing Questlines or content provided by members of staff
  • Running content inside of an area of the Forgotten Realms which a staff member is currently focused on

Initial Tame

Before training begins, the creature must first be made non-hostile through patience, calmness, or courage.

Roll Result
Natural 20 Start at 25% Bond Progress
Success Creature becomes non-hostile and open to training
Fail by 5 or less You fail to train the creature, you may retry after 1 day
Fail by 5 or more You fail to train the creature, you may retry after 3 days
Natural 1 The creature flees or attacks, you may retry after 1 week

Long-Term Bond Training

Each day requires 8 hours of focused interaction. You can train only one creature at a time. Each success adds your roll to a cumulative total.

Roll Result
Natural 20 Add roll plus 10% of total progress threshold
Success You Progress your bond with the creature
Fail by 5 or less Your bond with the creature does not progress
Fail by 5 or more The creature refuses training, you may retry after 3 days
Natural 1 The creature flees or attacks, you may retry after 1 week

Use of Tamed Creatures In Combat

The creature acts on its own initiative. You control it as you would a summon RAW from spells such as Conjure Animals.

Mounted Combat for Tamed Creatures

  • Because of the inherent danger of things such as meteor swarms, when you are mounted the creature shares your HP.
  • If you fall to zero HP, your mount falls to 1 HP, you are dismounted if on land, and if it is a flying creature it begins to crashland at 60ft/round.
  • For the purposes of movement, the mount replaces your move speed(s).
  • HP when dismounting: When you dismount, if your HP is lower than the mount's HP, the mount will adopt the same current HP as you.
  • If you choose to have the mount attack, take any action, or otherwise treat it as a separate creature, ignore points 1~4 and it will be treated as a normal mount RAW.
Mounted Combatant Feat interaction

Under this system you can use either RAW and risk damage to the mount or the alternative rules in the Animal Taming doc. If you're using the alternative rules only point 1 of the feat pertaining to advantaged against creatures is applicable, the other two are nullified.

Sale and Transfer of Animals

  • The recipient must be capable of hitting the initial tame DC to be capable of controlling the creature in any way.
  • By default, the bond level resets to zero. If you are proficient in Animal Handling, it resets to 25%. If you have expertise in Animal Handling, it resets to 50%

Tame Death

If your tamed creature dies and is revived it will regress one cumulative level. If your tame is regressed past 0 the taming process with restart requiring a new initial tame roll.

Prestige of the Gods

Prestige of the Gods

A character who has reached level 20 and achieved the 6 post-20 milestones may choose to undergo a divine transformation in which the gods revert their mind and body to the state of a neophyte adventurer. Starting again at level 1-3, a ‘prestiged’ adventurer may begin again - choosing any new class, feats, or even remodeling their own personal attributes. They retain all knowledge, wealth, and any items they had prior to this.

Upon choosing to undergo the transformation, the adventurer may choose a cosmetic effect to be bestowed upon them. This effect must be proposed by the player, and approved by a DM in the request to prestige the character (in #transcript). Examples of such an effect could include: Flittering sparkles that follow the character, an ethereal halo above their head, or a glowing/changing tattoo.

Paragon Systems: Gestalt and Ascendant

Gestalts are now considered a Paragon system, with the addition of Ascendants, which operate in a similar way - granting class levels past 20 - without having to reset.

A character’s Paragon stage represents the number of times they have completed a full class level system (finished p20), starting at a value of 1. This number is used to determine experience gain, and for communicating your character’s progress, as seen in “How are Levels Counted”. Such systems and changes can and will be subject to change and active monitoring.

How Levels are Counted

Levels are counted through the following format A#L#, where A represents your paragon stage, and L represents your remaining levels. For example, a P26 wizard with 10 additional levels from this system would be an A1L10. This can be similarly done with tiers.

This is similarly done with gestalt, in which the format is G#L#, where G represents your Paragon stage, and L represents your current level, up to 26. This can be similarly done with tiers.

Ascendant Paragon

Characters can now progress past level 20, continuing to gain character levels, being known as Ascendants rather than Gestalts. The character’s XP counter would reset to zero, though they would keep their character levels, proficiency bonus, and P20 rewards. Such Ascendants would proceed to level another class, starting from T1, and gaining an appropriate amount of XP, with the ability to do so again upon completing P20.

Build Rules

  • To qualify a PC must be fully leveled, meaning level 20 with all post 20 levels completed.

  • Ascendant PCs cannot gain experience from hunts, trials, DM-XP, Transcript-XP, etc. They can only gain experience from RP-XP or Quests.

  • In quests that involve combat, while you will receive XP for the RP, you will receive no XP for the creatures killed.

  • Ascendant hit dice are restricted to the two highest hit dice per level (as opposed to getting all hit dice per level).

  • Rather than gaining the save proficiencies of all classes, Ascendants choose one strong save and one weak save from the options given to them by their classes:

    • Strong Saves: Dex, Con, Wis
    • Weak Saves: Str, Int, Cha

  • Any changes to the locked classes or subclasses are allowed and follow the normal retraining rules.

  • Due to the documentation required for this system, character sheets must be handled in G-Sheets.

  • ASIs stack, but do not stack past the normal cap of 20 unless otherwise specified. Stats may not exceed 30 for any reason.

  • You can not take the same feat multiple times unless otherwise specified. Feats that scale based on level are calculated based on your class levels.

  • Ascendant PC's, May only hunt, trial or delve with PC's who are also Ascendant

  • Spell save DCs and spellcasting follow multi classing rules. Spell Save DCs and Spell Attack Bonuses calculate based on the class providing the spell.

  • Class features that references class levels are self contained for the applicable class and paragon cycle

Stacking

Class Features, Spell Slots, Proficiencies and Stat Changes generally stack unless otherwise specified.

Exceptions

The following features do NOT stack when creating a Ascendant PC:

  • Proficiency Bonus
  • Extra Attack
  • Unarmored Defense
  • Duplicated Paladin Auras
  • Movement speed increases from duplicate sources.
  • Bladesong, and other features with limited uses such as rage, do not get doubled buffs but do get a increased number of uses.

Prestige Classes

The Ascendant Paragon system also includes Prestige Classes, classes that have specific requirements for their use and play, but are generally stronger than the normal classes. Further information can be found at the Prestige Classes page.

Blessing of Forgiveness - This blessing removes one existing instance of permanent loss of wish. Once a creature has received this blessing, they can no longer receive it from the same deity.

Gestalt/Wish Patch:

tl;dr - Introducing a very arduous method to regain wish through a whole gestalt prestige and questline using a Post20 Blessing/Boon Slot.

Requirements

Gestalt prestige into a spellcasting class that also learns wish

Complete self RP and DM-led quests (at least 4) throughout your prestige that meet the following guidelines

Integrate with an existing group of worshipers for a particular deity

Receive character references from leadership within the church/group

Reach a level that would otherwise grant you wish from your prestige class

Travel to the chosen deity’s home plane and petition their aid directly with offerings and references prepared

Donate at least 2 items to the god directly, sacrificing them to prove your devotion. One item must be of legendary power and the second must be of great personal importance to your PC.

Perform a quest for that god directly, run by a Dungeonmaster and approved by at least 2 more DMs or LMs

Regaining Wish

Once all of the requirements have been met and your PC is eligible for a Post20 Boon/Blessing Slot, the God will decide whether or not to grant you the Blessing of Forgiveness:

Blessing of Forgiveness - This blessing removes one existing instance of permanent loss of wish. Once a creature has received this blessing, they can no longer receive it from the same deity.

Note: It may not be possible to convince any other gods to grant this blessing or you may attract significant attention from your previous god if so. It would require unfathomable time and effort to regain wish a second time.

Regarding Simulacrums

  1. Sims Simulacrums are considered NPCs. Paragons will not be able to earn rpxp and quest xp by way of proxy
